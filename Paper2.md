## Abstract

This paper specifies and defends a strict eliminative statistical protocol — the Conditional Biological Requirements Architecture (CBRA) — for testing whether complex biological state transitions, such as emergence from anesthesia, require organizational variables that live on the active boundary of the system and are not absorbed by a strong operational model of the system's measured dynamics. The architecture is conditional and falsifiable throughout: it is never offered as evidence that living tissue implements anything, and it is built so that a clean negative result is not only possible but expected unless a structured boundary residual survives an adversarial sequence of controls.

CBRA depends conceptually on a companion negative result — that classical control theory cannot model agency-preserving transformation — but it does not depend on the success of any biological mechanism, and the companion critique does not depend on CBRA. The architecture asks a single conditional question: *if* an identity-indexed, Lambda-like persistence channel were materially instantiated in living tissue, what would the tissue have to implement, and what residual structure would have to be measurable after the strongest available operational null model had already absorbed everything it could?

The paper makes four substantive corrections to the naive version of this program. First, it corrects a category error in the use of the Mori–Zwanzig projection: the physical boundary $E$ does not interact with the mathematical projection operator $\mathcal{P}$; rather, $E$ is folded into the classical microphysical substrate ($C \cup E$), and its biological role is to alter the Liouville generator of that substrate *before* projection, so that metabolic expenditure keeps the memory kernel produced by projection from collapsing. Second, it retreats from the indefensible claim that cardio-interoceptive variables are *constitutive* invariants of the psychological or historical self; the heart-transplant case forces their reclassification as proxies for the receptivity/orientation boundary, and that reclassification turns the transplant from a refutation into a confirmation — the self survives organ replacement, but the thermodynamic cost of re-anchoring and the texture of homeostasis change radically. Third, it demotes the computational demonstration from a false claim of spontaneous emergence to a "low-dimensional syntactic-consistency proof": the mode separation and geometric constraints were injected by hand to show the grammar is algorithmically executable and non-empty, nothing more. Fourth, it strengthens the residual-qualification layer with three explicit tests — relaxation asymmetry, idempotent covariance rotation, and multivariate IAAFT surrogate testing — designed to defeat the most serious neuroscientific objection, namely that any structured residual is merely long-tailed stochastic fluctuation of background neural noise.

CBRA does not claim that the brain implements such a channel. It specifies the empirical burden that any such claim would have to discharge, and the conditions under which the claim fails.

A proof of concept (section 15.5) addresses the architecture's principal empirical rival, self-organized criticality. It was intended to demonstrate that a genuinely boundary-gated system is separable from a matched non-gated critical system; direct testing instead shows the separation is not achieved by this construction. The stratified conditional-success differential it proposes as the separating statistic — which an additive generative process cannot produce — is nonetheless reproduced, at realistic parameters under a clean read-out and weakly homeostatic feedback, by a bare nonlinear critical generator, with magnitude exceeding the gating signal and sign set by an arbitrary read-out convention. The two upper criteria do not recover the discrimination in this model: the directional rung is structurally untestable, because modelling gating as multiplicative efficacy rescaling opens no sustained attractor for it to detect, and the generalization rung does not separate, because the critical leak's signature is invariant across network topology and stimulation by critical universality. The honest consequence is that the positive (detection) arm of the architecture is *not yet demonstrated to be feasible* in any instantiation offered here; the defensible contribution is the eliminative/negative arm. This corrects, rather than confirms, the naive form of the gating test.

**Keywords:** conditional biological requirements; eliminative statistics; operational null model; structured residuals; surrogate data testing; IAAFT; relaxation asymmetry; covariance rotation; non-holonomic constraint; Mori–Zwanzig; generalized Langevin equation; memory kernel; active boundary; Markov blankets; autopoiesis; organizational closure; anesthesia; consciousness-state transition; dirty Lambda; heart-brain axis; cardiac interoception; heart-rate variability; heartbeat-evoked potentials; heart transplant; receptivity boundary; out-of-sample prediction; model comparison.

**Architectural orientation.** CBRA is a model-comparison protocol, not a theory of consciousness or a defense of any molecule. Its entire content is a single dependency chain — from a pre-specified identity contract, to a projected macro-variable, to an active boundary, to a measured boundary vector, to a structurally qualified residual, to prospective out-of-sample prediction of a target transition — together with the conditions under which each link fails. The load-bearing element is not the prediction of the transition but the preservation, by an orthogonal and independently measured proxy, of a macro-invariant *through* the transition: this is the only point at which the protocol touches the non-branching constraint and thereby distinguishes a preserved trajectory from a reconstructed match. Everything else is scaffolding for, or controls on, that single test.

**Scope and non-claims.** Three delimitations hold throughout. First, CBRA is conditional and never evidential: nothing here is offered as evidence that tissue implements a Lambda-like channel, and the architecture is built so that a clean negative result is possible. Second, the cardio-interoceptive material treats cardiac and interoceptive variables as candidate components of a *receptivity boundary* whose residual contribution must survive a strong operational null model; it never treats the heart as a cognitive or identity-bearing organ, and section 10.3.1 makes the retreat from any such claim explicit. Third, the computational material (section 15) is a syntactic-consistency proof of a low-dimensional grammar; it establishes executability and non-vacuity, never biological actuality, and section 15.1 states this demotion without hedging.

## 1. Introduction: From a Negative Critique to an Eliminative Test

A companion paper establishes a negative, transcendental result: that classical control theory cannot model agency-preserving existential transformation without collapsing into tautology, annihilation, or incommensurable indeterminacy, and that what survives those exclusions is an admissibility profile — Class G — for externally sourced, receptivity-gated, mode-selective, identity-preserving transformation. That result is a constraint on any adequate *account* of transformation. It says nothing about whether any biological system instantiates the profile.

This paper asks the downstream conditional question, and asks it as a falsifiable empirical protocol rather than as a metaphysical thesis. Suppose, *conditionally*, that an identity-indexed, approximately Lambda-like persistence channel were materially realized in living neural tissue. What would the tissue have to implement — what boundary, repair, transduction, and non-redundancy architecture — and, decisively, what residual structure would have to be *measurable* after the strongest available operational model of the system's ordinary dynamics had already absorbed everything it could absorb? CBRA is the answer, stated as a model-comparison protocol with explicit no-go commitments.

The orientation of the protocol is eliminative throughout. CBRA is designed so that the default outcome — the outcome it predicts in the overwhelming majority of cases — is *negative*: that a candidate boundary residual is fully absorbed by the operational null model, or survives only as unmodeled physiology, measurement artifact, or long-tailed background fluctuation. A positive result requires that a structured boundary residual survive an adversarial sequence of controls of increasing severity (sections 3.2, 3.3, 12). The architecture earns the right to be taken seriously precisely by being easy to reject.

### 1.1 Dependence On, and Independence From, the Companion Critique

The dependency between this paper and the companion critique is one-directional. CBRA depends *conceptually* on the negative critique: the notion of a Lambda-like persistence channel, the constitutive/indicative distinction, the contract *I*, and the Class G admissibility profile are all imported from it. But CBRA's empirical fate does not feed back. The failure of every CBRA branch would not touch the companion result, which is a structural claim about the limits of control theory and stands or falls on its own argument. Symmetrically, the success of the companion critique licenses no biological claim; it only makes the conditional question of this paper well-posed. A reader may accept the negative critique and reject CBRA entirely, or accept CBRA's protocol as sound while remaining agnostic about the critique; the two are logically separable and are deliberately published as separable.

This separability must be stated more carefully than a slogan, because there is a real objection that CBRA is not a neutral protocol but a *rescue architecture* built solely to find physical room for Class G — so that if the trilemma's exhaustiveness fails, the whole motive for hunting a "structured residual outside classical control" collapses with it. The objection has a correct part and an incorrect part, and separating them is what the one-directional dependency actually means. The correct part: CBRA's *motivation* does depend on the companion result. If classical control could after all formulate identity-preserving transformation, there would be no philosophical reason to look for boundary structure non-redundant against an operational null, and the program would lose its point. This is conceded; CBRA is not motivationally neutral, and it would be dishonest to present it as though it fell from the sky. The incorrect part: the *internal validity of the eliminative protocol* does not depend on the trilemma at all. The model comparison — does a structurally qualified, independently measured boundary residual add non-redundant out-of-sample predictive value over a strong operational null? — is a well-posed empirical question whose answer is the same whatever one thinks of Class G. If the trilemma were entirely wrong, the protocol would still correctly return whether boundary residuals are redundant; it would simply have lost the reason anyone cared to run it. So CBRA is a rescue architecture in its *motivation* and a neutral eliminative test in its *machinery*, and its design is such that the machinery is built to destroy the very thing the motivation hopes to find. That is the sense in which it is not question-begging: a rescue architecture that is engineered to return, and is expected to return, a negative result is not chasing a foregone conclusion. If Class G is in the end logically inconsistent, CBRA does not thereby "hunt statistical ghosts"; it returns the negative it was built to be able to return, and the cost is borne by the motivation, not by the test.

### 1.2 What CBRA Success Would and Would Not Establish

A maximally successful CBRA result — a structurally qualified boundary residual that prospectively and non-redundantly predicts an $I$-supporting transition while an orthogonal proxy shows the macro-invariant preserved through the transition — would establish exactly one thing: that a measured boundary process contributes to the preservation or recovery of an *I*-relevant macro-invariant in a way not reducible to the operational null. It would *not* establish that the boundary process is personal identity, psychological continuity, relational history, consciousness, or the soul. The preservation of relational, narrative, and responsibility-bearing identity remains governed by the higher-level constraints of the contract *I*; it is not established by firing-rate recovery, state transition, or boundary prediction alone. The most a positive result reaches is the *biological precondition*: whether the boundary architecture protects the functional projection required for an identity-bearing trajectory to resume at all.

**A positive success criterion that does not depend on instrument resolution.** A fair objection to the whole architecture is that its positive arm is hostage to measurement: section 7.2 concedes that a sufficiently spatially-resolved metabolic-configurational null would absorb $\varepsilon^b_t$, and section 12.3 concedes that a criticality-complete null might do the same, so it can look as though CBRA can only ever return "negative" or "not yet resolvable" and never a clean positive. This would make the program a perpetually deferred promise rather than a falsifiable architecture, and the charge must be met with a success criterion that is *structural* rather than *resolution-bounded*. There is one, and it is the load-bearing test the architecture already names: the *boundary-gating interaction* conjoined with *orthogonal invariant preservation through the transition* (sections 8.1, 13 rungs 5–6). The interaction term $u_t\times\varepsilon^b_t$ tests whether the *effect of an external perturbation depends on the boundary state* — a conditional-dependence structure that a higher-resolution null does not dissolve, because resolving the boundary geometry more finely does not make the perturbation's efficacy cease to be gated by it; it only redescribes the gate. A criticality-derived or configurational null that fully predicts $\varepsilon^b_t$ would absorb the *additive* boundary contribution, but a genuine *interaction* — the perturbation working only when the boundary residual is in a particular state, and the macro-invariant surviving the transition in the generator-subtracted residual — is a different functional form that an additive null of any resolution does not reproduce. The honest positive criterion is therefore not "a residual survives an ever-finer null" (which is resolution-bounded and possibly unreachable) but "the gating interaction and orthogonal preservation hold jointly after the strongest available additive null, including criticality and configurational predictors, has been included." That criterion can be met or fail at *achievable* resolution: it does not require out-resolving the configurational null, only demonstrating a conditional-dependence structure the additive null cannot represent regardless of its resolution. If even the gating interaction is reproducible by a sufficiently rich nonlinear-but-additive critical generator, CBRA fails outright — but that is a finite, performable test, not an infinite regress against instrument precision. This is the sense in which the architecture is built to be *positively* rejectable and not merely deferred. The exact form this test must take is corrected in section 15.5: the conditional-dependence structure is *not* reliably read off an interaction regressor in a flexible classifier (which an over-parameterized additive null absorbs even under strong coupling), but *is* read off the stratified conditional-success differential of a manipulable perturbation under a matched non-gating control, which an additive generative process cannot produce; a bare nonlinear critical generator, however, can reproduce it at realistic parameters under a clean read-out and weakly homeostatic feedback (section 15.5), so the differential is necessary but not sufficient and would have to be conjoined with the directional barrier-lowering and generalization rungs to discriminate genuine gating. Direct testing of those upper rungs (section 15.5) does not, in the proof-of-concept model, recover the discrimination either. The honest status is therefore the one the abstract states: the criterion remains *positively rejectable* — CBRA can and does return a negative when no inabsorbable structure survives — but its *positive* arm is not yet demonstrated to be feasible in any instantiation offered here, and the architecture's secure contribution is eliminative.

## 2. From Formal Persistence to Biological Burden

Part of the burden is thermodynamic and holds independently of any specific mechanism. If identity-like persistence is embodied at all, it cannot be the passive possession of a stable object; it must be actively maintained against thermalization, perturbation, turnover, and boundary leakage. A living system persists by maintaining a boundary between internal organization and external disorder — described in theoretical biology through organizational closure, autopoiesis, physiological individuality, and Markov blankets (Maturana and Varela 1980; Mossio, Saborido, and Moreno 2009; Friston 2010, 2013; Pradeu 2012, 2016; Palacios et al. 2020). In the present framework, a boundary is required for *projected noise*: it lets the system remain open to the world without becoming transparent to destructive noise.

Embodied persistence therefore requires boundary maintenance, energy expenditure, repair below an error threshold, separation between identity-bearing and agency-bearing degrees of freedom, resistance to thermalization, and capacity for adaptive interaction. This corresponds to the distinction $D_{ag} > 0$, $D_{id} < D_c$: agency-bearing diffusion preserved, identity-bearing diffusion held below the destruction threshold. This boundary cost is not borne by neural tissue alone — cardiac rhythm, respiration, vascular tone, vagal signaling, and interoceptive prediction form part of the organism's active boundary economy, regulating how perturbations become arousal, attention, or adaptive readiness rather than destructive noise. The biological question of this paper follows: not whether a brain contains exact Lambda dynamics, but what living tissue would have to implement if any approximate Lambda-like persistence channel were biologically realized.

### 2.1 CBRA as Conditional Self-Maintenance Architecture

The biological core of CBRA should be read through autopoiesis, organizational closure, active inference, and physiological individuality before any high-risk molecular branch (Maturana and Varela 1980; Mossio, Saborido, and Moreno 2009; Friston 2010, 2013; Pradeu 2012, 2016). Its primary claim is not that a molecule carries identity. It is that any biological implementation of Lambda-like persistence must actively maintain a boundary, regulate destructive perturbation, repair below threshold, and preserve a macro-invariant through component turnover. CBRA is a theory of biological persistence *conditions*, not a sufficient theory of consciousness; a system satisfying every CBRA condition would still require a separate account of how boundary-mediated persistence contributes to experience.

## 3. The Operational Null Model and the Non-Redundancy Condition

The relevant competitor is not a weak null model. It is a *strong operational null model*:

> Consciousness-state transitions, memory stability, and self-model continuity are fully explained by measured neural, metabolic, pharmacological, homeostatic, active-inference-compatible, Markov-blanket-compatible, and attractor-dynamical variables.

This is essential. Active inference and Markov-blanket theory are not treated as variables in the abstract; they enter the null only through operational predictors: firing rates, membrane excitability, EEG/MEG features, effective connectivity, receptor occupancy, pharmacological dose, metabolic variables, hemodynamic drift, respiratory phase, autonomic markers, cardiac variables, interoceptive proxies, temperature, mitochondrial state, and explicitly operationalized proxies for precision, prediction error, policy entropy, or boundary-mediated self-maintenance. Because self-organized criticality is the leading non-boundary explanation of structured transitional cortical dynamics (section 12.3), the strong null must *also* include criticality-derived predictors wherever they can be estimated — neuronal-avalanche size and duration statistics, the branching parameter, and distance-to-criticality or susceptibility measures — so that a boundary residual is never credited for structure a critical generator would have produced. The most demanding form of the null — the one against which the thermodynamic objection of section 7.2 is answered — additionally includes whatever *configurational* variables fixing boundary geometry (local cytoskeletal architecture, membrane-domain organization, channel spatial distribution) can be independently resolved. This is the strongest competitor CBRA faces, and the architecture's wager is precisely that the boundary-organizational structure carried by $\varepsilon^b_t$ is not exhausted by the criticality-derived and configurational proxies a null can presently resolve; if it is, CBRA fails (sections 7.2, 12.3). This measured set is $N^{op}_t$.

The inclusion is conservative: cardiac variables do not count as CBRA evidence merely because they correlate with attention, emotion, or state transitions. Heart-rate variability, baroreflex sensitivity, cardiac phase, respiratory sinus arrhythmia, heartbeat-evoked potentials, and cardiorespiratory coupling belong *first* to the operational null. Only their residual — if independently measured, structurally qualified, prospectively predictive, and non-redundant — can support a distinct cardio-interoceptive branch.

CBRA has empirical value only if independently measured boundary-state variables contain structure not absorbed by $N^{op}_t$. The non-redundancy condition is residual and operational. A model including boundary residuals must improve out-of-sample predictive performance over the pre-specified baseline:

$$\text{Score}\big(M_1 : S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^b_t\big) > \text{Score}\big(M_0 : S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t\big).$$

Here $S^I_{t+1}$ is an $I$-supporting transition — anesthetic induction, emergence, sleep-wake transition, recovery of responsiveness — not a direct proof of personal identity. Suitable metrics include expected log predictive density, cross-validated log likelihood, WAIC, Bayes factors, or classification accuracy, declared before data collection and applied out of sample. The decisive question is never whether boundaries exist — they do — but whether a structured boundary residual improves prediction after the strongest operational null has already been included. CBRA is not a rival to active inference at the same level; active inference supplies part of the null, and CBRA is non-redundant only if narrower boundary-maintenance variables improve prediction after residualization against $N^{op}_t$.

### 3.1 Operational Examples of the Null Model, Boundary Vector, and Target Transition

| Variable class | Examples | Role in model comparison |
|---|---|---|
| $S^I_{t+1}$ | Loss or return of responsiveness; induction/emergence time; sleep-wake transition; coma recovery; global-availability transition; population-level state shift. | $I$-supporting target outcome to be predicted. |
| $N^{op}_t$ | Receptor occupancy; synaptic markers; firing rates; membrane excitability; EEG/MEG features; effective connectivity; metabolic stress; mitochondrial markers; neuromodulation; glial variables; pharmacological dose; temperature; hemodynamic drift; respiratory phase; operational active-inference or Markov-blanket proxies. | Strong operational baseline. |
| $b_t$ | Microtubule polymerization/stability state ($MPS_t$); membrane-cytoskeleton coupling ($MCC_t$). | Physical boundary states measured independently of the outcome. |
| $\varepsilon^b_t$ | Residual boundary component after predicting $b_t$ from $N^{op}_t$. | Candidate signal; not evidence unless structurally qualified. |
| $h_t$ | HRV, baroreflex sensitivity, cardiac phase, RSA, HEP, cardiorespiratory coupling. | Candidate cardio-interoceptive vector; enters first as part of $N^{op}_t$ unless a residual component is independently measured and non-redundant. |

The cardio-interoceptive residual $\varepsilon^h_t = h_t - \widehat{h}_t(N^{op}_t)$ becomes CBRA-relevant only if it improves out-of-sample prediction of $S^I_{t+1}$ after respiratory, hemodynamic, pharmacological, neural, metabolic, and measurement confounds are controlled.

### 3.2 What Would Count as a CBRA-Negative Result?

CBRA is designed to be rejectable. The following, if met, constitute negative evidence of increasing severity.

**Redundancy failure.** If $\varepsilon^b_t$ improves prediction only marginally and the improvement disappears after controlling for known $N^{op}_t$ variables, the boundary contribution is a relabeling of ordinary physiology.

**Absorption failure.** If the predictive content of $b_t$ is fully absorbed into $N^{op}_t$ in nested comparisons, CBRA earns no independent empirical role.

**Confound failure.** If boundary variables predict $S^I_{t+1}$ only because they track toxicity, metabolic stress, ATP availability, motor impairment, inflammation, hemodynamic drift, respiratory phase, circadian phase, temperature drift, or instrumental drift already in $N^{op}_t$, the predictive value is spurious.

**Slow-wave failure.** If the residual shows temporal persistence only because unmodeled slow oscillations remain — respiration, circadian phase, hemodynamics, vascular drift, thermal drift, motion artifact, metabolic cycling — it is not dirty-Lambda evidence.

**Criticality failure.** If the residual's structure — its heavy tails, its regime change, its eigenframe reorganization, and even its prospective predictive value — is reproduced by a self-organized-criticality or near-critical cortical generator once avalanche statistics, the branching parameter, and distance-to-criticality measures are included in the null, then the boundary contribution is redundant with ordinary critical dynamics and CBRA earns no independent role. This is the most probable way a structured residual that passes the three tests of section 12 nonetheless fails, because criticality generically produces all three signatures (section 12.3).

**Non-physiological failure.** If boundary effects on membrane excitability, channel probability, or synchrony appear only under artificial overexpression, extreme fields, non-physiological illumination, or toxic perturbation, the branch fails to show physiological relevance.

**Transduction failure.** If boundary variables show no measurable effect on drift terms, barrier heights, transition rates, or synchrony, the architecture is epiphenomenal — an invariant with no causal path is a label.

**Generalization and feasibility failure.** If predictive improvement does not generalize across preparations, subjects, species, perturbation classes, or platforms, the result is parochial. If the variables cannot be measured with sufficient resolution to remain independent of $N^{op}_t$, CBRA remains a requirements architecture without empirical foothold.

A single negative result of sufficient weight in any category should prompt retraction or severe revision of the relevant branch. If all testable branches fail, CBRA fails. A framework that cannot be rejected is not a requirements architecture; it is speculation dressed as structure.

### 3.3 Residual Humility: Why Structured Residuals Are Not Yet Evidence

A structured boundary residual is not, by itself, evidence for CBRA. It is first a *model-discrepancy signal*: the most conservative reading of $\varepsilon^b_t$ is that the null model failed to absorb some structured variance, which may reflect unmeasured nonlinear physiology, hidden metabolic coupling, glial dynamics, unmapped cytoskeletal effects, local feedback, slow oscillatory structure, instrumentation artifacts, or an omitted interaction among ordinary variables. CBRA-positive interpretation requires a stricter adversarial sequence: pre-specification (the contract *I* and target $S^I_{t+1}$ locked before inspecting boundary variables; post hoc selection disqualifying); independent measurement (boundary channels orthogonal to the outcome metrics); out-of-sample gain (cross-validated improvement beyond an optimized $N^{op}_t$ by a pre-registered margin); confound elimination (survival of penalization for slow-wave, metabolic, hemodynamic, respiratory, thermal, and drift covariates, and for operational active-inference proxies); perturbational tractability (causal manipulation shifts the transition probability in the predicted direction, not merely correlates); and generalization (the effect holds across preparations, subjects, perturbation classes, or platforms). Failure at any stage demotes the result. Only a pre-specified, structurally qualified, perturbationally tractable, generalizing residual counts as provisional evidence — and section 12 adds three further tests aimed specifically at the stochastic-fluctuation objection.

## 4. Independence of Boundary Variables

The strongest objection to CBRA is redundancy. A candidate boundary-state vector $b_t$ satisfies the independence requirement only if it meets four conditions simultaneously: (i) it is measured by a channel not trivially identical to the $N^{op}_t$ measure it is meant to improve; (ii) its residual $\varepsilon^b_t$ predicts $S^I_{t+1}$ *before* the transition occurs, not merely correlates afterward; (iii) its contribution survives controls for dose, receptor action, firing rate, synaptic transmission, metabolism, toxicity, hemodynamics, respiratory phase, temperature, network state, and operational active-inference or Markov-blanket proxies; and (iv) it generalizes across preparations, subjects, or perturbation classes. Condition (ii) is the one most easily missed: a boundary variable that co-varies with a transition but cannot predict it in advance is a correlate of a correlate, not a CBRA boundary variable.

The residualization must not destroy the structure it tests. The null should not indiscriminately absorb every covariate; slow confounds should be modeled through pre-specified reduced-dimensional or regularized representations — principal components of hemodynamic and respiratory drift, penalized regression of collinear metabolic predictors — so that $\varepsilon^b_t$ is not artificially collapsed into white noise by overfitting. The aim is not to protect the residual from serious controls but to prevent an overparameterized null from converting all structured biology into statistical debris. For cardio-interoceptive variables, independence additionally requires that $h_t$ not be a disguised measure of respiration, hemodynamic drift, arousal, dose, or movement: ECG-derived, respiration-derived, and EEG-derived heartbeat-evoked components must be separated by acquisition channel, preprocessing pipeline, and temporal window, and a heartbeat-evoked potential contaminated by cardiac field artifact or by the same EEG feature used to define $S^I_{t+1}$ cannot count as independent.

## 5. Approximate and Dirty-Lambda Biology

Exact Lambda-regime persistence is a mathematical ideal. Living tissue cannot implement exact quasi-periodicity, exact tensor-network isometry, exact quantum error correction, or exact integrability (Beny and Oreshkov 2010; Pastawski et al. 2015; Cao and Lackey 2021; Cao, Pollack, and Wang 2022). A biological model uses an approximate condition. Let $x_t$ be the microscopic state, $X_t = \phi(x_t)$ a coarse-grained macrostate, and *I* an identity contract. A system approximates Lambda-like persistence if

$$\liminf_{T\to\infty} \frac{1}{T}\int_0^T |C_{I\phi}(t)|^2\,dt \;\geq\; P_{min} > 0,$$

while allowing continuous microscopic variation, turnover, and local damage — approximated in practice by a pre-registered finite-window statistic over biologically justified timescales. The criterion is macro-invariant retention under microstate replacement, not microscopic preservation. A biological implementation therefore requires a coarse-grained identity-bearing channel, an error threshold below which defects are repaired, a repair rate faster than defect percolation, a boundary separating identity-bearing degrees of freedom from destructive noise, and a transduction path into classical neural dynamics. This is *dirty Lambda*: approximate, metabolically maintained, error-thresholded persistence. Cardiac rhythm is a paradigmatic dirty-Lambda case — structured variability, multiscale correlation, respiratory coupling, autonomic modulation — provided no inference is drawn from cardiac complexity to personal identity without the intervening *I*-indexed constraints (Goldberger 2006).

The move to an approximate condition carries a real and self-inflicted danger that must be named, because it is the kind of move that destroys falsifiability if left unguarded. By defining biological Lambda as approximate, noisy, and fault-tolerant, the architecture makes the hypothesis immune to refutation by *dynamical deviation*: any observed departure from exact quasi-periodicity, any drift, any local failure of memory can be reabsorbed as "expected dirtiness," so that no dynamical measurement of the signal *by itself* can ever falsify the claim. This is conceded in full. The consequence is that the entire falsificatory burden is transferred off the dynamical signature and onto the model comparison — and this transfer is deliberate, not evasive, but it only works if the model comparison is genuinely capable of returning a negative. "Dirty Lambda" earns its place only because the architecture nowhere licenses inferring a Lambda channel *from the dynamical signature*; the signature (bounded, aperiodic, correlation-retaining, error-thresholded) is treated as necessary and radically insufficient, and the decisive test is always whether a structurally qualified boundary residual adds non-redundant out-of-sample prediction over a strong operational null that already contains nonlinear and attractor-dynamical predictors. If the term "dirty Lambda" were ever used to license a positive inference from the signal's appearance — "it looks structured-but-variable, therefore it is a Lambda channel" — that would be exactly the unfalsifiable move the critic rightly fears, and it is prohibited throughout. The approximation is admissible only on the condition that it shifts the proof obligation to the eliminative model comparison and never functions as a free pass for the signal.

## 6. Projected Noise and the Active Boundary Requirement

The distinction between agency-bearing and identity-destroying diffusion must hold biologically. Near an identity-bearing manifold $M_\Lambda$, decompose the relevant degrees of freedom $TM_\Lambda = E_{id}\oplus E_{ag}$. Biological agency and adaptation require active fluctuations $D_{ag} > 0$; identity-bearing modes must stay below threshold $D_{id} < D_c$. A viable biological Lambda cannot require zero noise (that produces rigidity), but if noise acts freely on identity-bearing modes, memory is lost. The system must allow soft exploratory modes while protecting hard identity modes — which requires an *active boundary* $E$ performing five functions: shielding, sensing, repair, transduction, and energy expenditure. A passive protected subsystem in warm tissue is implausible; the relevant question is whether metabolism can maintain boundary conditions that preserve a coarse-grained invariant while allowing measurable neural expression. In cardio-interoceptive systems, projected noise appears as the difference between adaptive autonomic variability and destructive dysregulation: HRV, RSA, and baroreflex dynamics matter only when they index *structured openness* rather than raw variance.

## 7. The Q-E-C Architecture and the Mori–Zwanzig Category Correction

### 7.1 The Tripartite Model

CBRA contains three components. $Q_I(t)$ is *not* a hidden substrate, biological compartment, or molecular carrier; it is the projected macro-variable $Q_I(t) = \psi_I(C_t)$ extracted from the classical, multiscale substrate under the contract *I* — the declared invariant to be tracked, not an entity that interacts with the substrate. $E_t$ denotes the operational filtering, memory-management, and projected-noise-regulation structure that maintains the stability of $Q_I$ under coarse-graining; it is not a wall, substance, or hidden interface. $C_t$ denotes the classical neural substrate: membrane potentials, oscillatory dynamics, firing rates, global workspace, policy selection, self-modeling. The architecture is $\Psi_I(t) = (Q_I(t), E_t, C_t)$.

### 7.2 Correcting the Category Error: E Lives in the Substrate, Not Beside the Projection

The naive version of this architecture contains a category error that must be corrected before any of it can be coherent. Because $Q_I$ is a projection of $C$ rather than a separate degree of freedom, the relation between them is not a physical coupling specified by an interaction Hamiltonian; it is the relation between a coarse-grained observable and the substrate it is extracted from, whose natural form is the Mori–Zwanzig generalized Langevin equation:

$$\frac{d}{dt}Q_I(C_t) = \mathcal{P}_I F(C_t) + \int_0^t K_I(t-s)\,Q_I(C_s)\,ds + \eta_I(t),$$

where $\mathcal{P}_I$ is the projection operator onto the *I*-relevant subspace, $F$ generates the substrate dynamics, $K_I$ is a memory kernel, and $\eta_I$ is projected (orthogonal-dynamics) noise.

The error to be undone is the treatment of the physical, molecular/biological boundary $E$ (microtubules, membrane coupling) and the *mathematical* projection operator $\mathcal{P}_I$ as entities that interact physically — as though a biological structure could push on a projection operator, or a projection operator could exert force on tissue. These are different categories: $\mathcal{P}_I$ is an operation on the description, while $E$ is part of the world being described. The correction is to fold the physical boundary into the classical microphysical substrate itself. $E$ is not beside the projection; $E \subseteq C$, so the structured substrate is $C \cup E$, and the projection $\mathcal{P}_I$ acts on the *total structured substrate*, not on a system from which $E$ has been mathematically separated.

The biological role of $E$ is then stated without category confusion. $E$ alters the *Liouville generator of the substrate*, $\mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}$, **before** the projection is taken. That is, the boundary changes the microphysical equations of motion of the substrate — through conformational bias, membrane tension, channel-state propensity, cytoskeletal stiffness — and these altered equations of motion are *then* projected. This active sustaining can be written explicitly as an endogenous thermodynamic flux term added to the substrate's generator, $\mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}(t) = \mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}^{0} + \mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$, where $\mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$ parameterizes the local, ATP-funded metabolic work that maintains boundary structure. Two properties of this term must be stated precisely, because they are what keep it from either committing a category error or reopening the thermodynamic objection below. First, $\mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$ is strictly *endogenous to the classical substrate*: it is internal to $C$ (the metabolism is the cell's own, $E \subseteq C$), and it acts on the generator *upstream* of the projection operator $P$ — it deforms the classical phase-space trajectories, it does not couple $Q_I$ to $C$ across a wall, and it adds no external energy reservoir. Second, because it acts before projection, $\mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$ shapes the non-local memory kernel $K(s)$ and the projected-noise statistics that the projection inherits: it is what holds $K(s)$ against thermal decay and pushes phase-destroying noise into the orthogonal subspace $Q$. Because the projection inherits its memory kernel $K_I$ and its projected-noise statistics $\eta_I$ from the generator it is applied to, an $E$ that shapes $\mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}$ thereby shapes $K_I$ and the statistics of $\eta_I$. The load-bearing biological claim is now precise and free of the category error: metabolic expenditure at the boundary, entering as $\mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$, maintains the structure of $\mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}$ such that the memory kernel produced by the projection does *not collapse*. In a closed equilibrium system the projected noise may be orthogonal to the relevant subspace by construction; in a living, open, far-from-equilibrium system it is not, and without active management $\eta_I$ becomes destructive diffusion relative to the declared invariant. "Boundary management" is load-bearing in this conditional sense — not because $Q_I$ and $C$ are coupled across a wall, but because the memory and projected-noise structure of an open system does not keep the declared macro-variable stable on its own, and the active, metabolically funded boundary is what sustains the kernel that does. Note that introducing $\mathcal{L}_{meta}(t)$ does not weaken the falsification condition stated below: because the term is endogenous and metabolic, a *spatially-resolved* metabolic null can in principle absorb its signature, and the architecture concedes exactly this (the resolution distinction that follows). The term formalizes the mechanism; it does not exempt it from the null.

The model is therefore neither direct physical interaction (which the category error wrongly imputes) nor isolation. It is a statement about which observable is tracked and what — namely a metabolically maintained boundary acting on the substrate's generator prior to projection — regulates its projected dynamics. Boundary variables become testable only insofar as they affect the classical dynamics from which $Q_I$ is projected.

This formulation invites a sharp thermodynamic objection. If $E \subseteq C$ and the energy that maintains the kernel is internal to the substrate — ATP hydrolysis at cytoskeletal and membrane structures — then a strong operational null $N^{op}_t$ that already includes mitochondrial markers, ATP availability, and classical ionic fluxes should, *by conservation of energy*, fully absorb the variance of $E$. For the residual $\varepsilon^b_t$ to survive a metabolically complete null, the energy sustaining the kernel would seem to require an external reservoir uncoupled from the measured metabolic variables — which would either violate energy conservation or smuggle in a mystical reservoir dressed as statistical physics. The objection is correct that no external energy reservoir may be invoked, and the architecture invokes none. A tempting reply — that $\varepsilon^b_t$ carries the *geometry* into which energy is spent rather than the *scalar magnitude* of energy spent, as two engines may burn identical fuel while delivering different torque — must be rejected as stated, because in cell biology it does not hold. Boundary geometry is not independent of the spatial map of dissipation: changing the wiring (microtubule polymerization, membrane-domain reorganization, the imposition of a non-holonomic constraint) requires molecular motors — kinesin, dynein — hydrolyzing ATP at specific spatial coordinates. The geometry *is* the spatiotemporal distribution of energy dissipation. A clean separation between "geometric information" and "energetic expenditure" is a blackboard abstraction that the tissue does not honor.

What survives the objection is therefore not a magnitude/geometry distinction but a *resolution* distinction, and stating it correctly makes the falsification condition sharper, not weaker. A metabolically complete null in the weak sense — a *scalar or globally aggregated* energy budget (total ATP turnover, bulk mitochondrial state) — does not fix boundary geometry and so does not by itself absorb $\varepsilon^b_t$. But a metabolically complete null in the strong sense — *spatially and temporally resolved* metabolic fluxes (local oxygen-consumption rates, mitochondrial-membrane-potential gradients, motor-protein activity maps) — *is* a configurational null: by the identity just stated, resolving the local dissipation map to sufficient precision is resolving the geometry, and such a null would absorb $\varepsilon^b_t$ through its local energetic signature. The two are the same null at high resolution. CBRA concedes this without reservation: against a spatially-resolved metabolic-configurational null, the residual must either survive or the architecture fails.

This states the falsification condition in its strongest form. CBRA's wager is precise and falsifiable: that there is boundary-organizational structure carried by $\varepsilon^b_t$ which is not recoverable even from the spatially-resolved local dissipation map a configurationally complete null would supply, while still being causally transductive into $C$. If a spatially-resolved metabolic-configurational null fully predicts $\varepsilon^b_t$, the architecture fails, because the boundary's organization was recoverable from classical local flux after all. The architecture therefore claims no energy from nowhere and no information immune to measurement; it claims, conditionally, that boundary organization at the relevant scale outruns the configurational resolution presently achievable — and it concedes, as the exact condition of its own defeat, that a null resolving local dissipation to the geometry's own scale would close the gap. Two consequences follow and are stated plainly. First, the residual's survival is bounded by instrumentation: CBRA is a claim about structure not captured at currently achievable configurational resolution, not a claim about structure unmeasurable *in principle*, and the distinction between those two is exactly what separates a risky empirical hypothesis from an untestable one (section 1.1). Second, the burden this places on a positive result is heavier than a bare residual: a surviving $\varepsilon^b_t$ must be shown non-redundant against the most spatially-resolved metabolic and configurational null the measurement state of the art allows, not merely against bulk metabolic scalars, and a result that has only cleared the weak null has cleared nothing of interest.

The dependency chains for the two implementation routes are then strictly parallel and subordinate, both indexed by the same *I* and tested against the same null:

$$I \Rightarrow Q_I \Rightarrow E \Rightarrow b_t \Rightarrow \varepsilon^b_t \Rightarrow S^I_{t+1}, \qquad I \Rightarrow Q_I \Rightarrow E_{cardio} \Rightarrow h_t \Rightarrow \varepsilon^h_t \Rightarrow S^I_{t+1}.$$

### 7.3 What the Projected Macro-Variable Can and Cannot Mean

$Q$ is not an unconstrained hidden substrate; in this architecture $Q$ means $Q_I$, the projected macro-variable of the constitutive invariants the contract *I* has *already* specified. The order of dependence is strict: *I* is not inferred from $Q$; $Q$ is admissible only insofar as it realizes, preserves, or indexes invariants *I* has fixed. A candidate $Q$ is not acceptable merely because it is stable, exotic, long-lived, topological, or quantum; it is acceptable only if its loss, disruption, or uncontrolled branching would correspond to a failure of the *I*-defined trajectory. Conversely, a structure that can vary, disappear, duplicate, or be replaced without affecting the *I*-relevant persistence claim is not $Q_I$ but at most an indicative variable. Measured boundary variables ($MPS_t$, $MCC_t$, components of $b_t$) do *not* measure $Q_I$; they measure the physical state of the boundary $E$. Empirical tests of CBRA are therefore tests of *boundary capacity*, not direct observations of identity.

| Candidate realization of $Q_I$ | What it would preserve | Why it may fail |
|---|---|---|
| Correlational $Q_I$ | Long-memory correlation structure relevant to *I*. | Correlation persists statistically but not as an *I*-constitutive invariant. |
| Topological/logical $Q_I$ | Protected invariant whose disruption tracks identity-relevant collapse. | Topological stability may be real but irrelevant to *I*. |
| Boundary-state ensemble $Q_I$ | Coarse-grained boundary-conditioned state preserving the *I*-relevant macro-invariant. | May be fully absorbed by $N^{op}_t$. |
| Spin-sensitive $Q_I$ | Spin/radical-pair/isotope/CISS-linked dynamics, only if they preserve or transduce an *I*-constitutive invariant. | Usually fails by scale, thermal noise, co-localization, amplification, or irrelevance to *I*. |

The conservative realizations (correlational, topological, boundary-state ensemble) are the default; the spin-sensitive branches are the highest-risk and are suspended to Appendix A, so CBRA cannot survive by migrating $Q_I$ from a failed conservative interpretation to an exotic one after the fact.

### 7.4 The Boundary as Active Markov Blanket

A Markov blanket separates internal from external states through sensory and active states; in CBRA, $E$ plays the analogous role for the *projection*, regulating which degrees of freedom of $C$ remain organized as the *I*-relevant macro-variable rather than dispersed into the rest of the substrate. The term is used cautiously: a Markov blanket is not a stand-alone metaphysical boundary of the person but a model for mediated coupling. Writing $C^\perp_t$ for the complementary degrees of freedom, the boundary enforces approximate conditional independence at the relevant scale, $Q_I(t) \perp_\approx C^\perp_t \mid E_t$ — not that $Q_I$ is a separate shielded entity (it is a projection of $C$) but that the projected macro-variable stays organized only when $E$ regulates the memory and projected-noise terms. Standard active-inference accounts are treated as *part of the operational null*. CBRA's additional claim is narrower: if an *I*-defined Lambda-like channel is to be tracked through $Q_I$, then $E$ must do more than mediate generic organism-environment coupling — it must keep $Q_I$ organized against destructive diffusion, repair below threshold, and transduce boundary-conditioned structure into classical dynamics in a way that improves prediction of $S^I_{t+1}$ beyond operational Markov-blanket and active-inference proxies. If that residual contribution is absent, CBRA is not an extension of Markov-blanket theory; it is redundant and fails.

## 8. Effective Transduction into Classical Dynamics

The classical substrate $C$ is not acted on by $Q_I$ as a separate entity; the projection $Q_I = \psi_I(C_t)$ constrains which boundary-conditioned features of $C$ count as persistence-relevant. To make the architecture operational, boundary influence must be expressible as an effective force on classical variables. Let $x_C$ be a low-dimensional classical coordinate (membrane excitability, channel-state probability, local field phase, cytoskeletal stiffness). A minimal overdamped effective dynamics is

$$\gamma\frac{dx_C}{dt} = F_{internal}(x_C) - \kappa\,\nabla_{x_C} D(x_C, E_{Q_I}) + \eta(t),$$

where $F_{internal}$ represents ordinary classical neural and biochemical forces, $D(x_C, E_{Q_I})$ is a phenomenological alignment/dissonance functional, $\kappa$ parameterizes the strength with which the boundary-conditioned projection affects classical drift, and $\eta(t)$ is thermal/biochemical noise. The term $-\kappa\nabla_{x_C} D$ is not a vital force and not direct $Q_I$-$C$ action; it is the coarse-grained force generated when the active boundary modulates the classical landscape — consistent with section 7.2, it is the downstream signature of $E$ having shaped $\mathcal{L}_{C\cup E}$. If boundary variables affect membrane or cytoskeletal variables, they must change drift terms, noise statistics, barrier heights, or transition rates; if no measurable change in effective drift or transition probability can be found, the boundary-transduction branch fails.

### 8.1 Biological Class G: Boundary-Gated Transformation

Biological Class G is not a second analogy added to the formal grammar; it is the dissipative embodiment of the same admissibility profile. A transformation counts as identity-preserving only if it lowers the barrier from a dispersive regime toward Lambda-like persistence while keeping the *I*-constitutive projection below its destruction threshold and preserving adaptive degrees of freedom. The operational signature is not that an external perturbation affects a future transition, nor that boundary variables predict a transition, but that the *effect of the perturbation is gated by the structured residual state of the boundary*. The model sequence:

$$\begin{aligned} M_0 &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t, \\ M_1 &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^b_t, \\ M_A &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + u_t + \varepsilon^b_t, \\ M_G &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + u_t + \varepsilon^b_t + (u_t \times \varepsilon^b_t). \end{aligned}$$

Operational biological G-admissibility requires a *cumulative* test, not a single statistical result, and is an operational laboratory criterion, not an ontological equivalence:

$$\text{Operational Biological G-Admissibility} \;\triangleq\; \Big(\Delta_{\text{pred}}(M_G, M_A) > \delta_{\min} \;\land\; \Delta V^{eff}_{M\to\Lambda}\downarrow\Big) \;\land\; D^{eff}_{id} < D_c \;\land\; D^{eff}_{ag} > 0,$$

where $\Delta_{\text{pred}}(M_G, M_A) = \text{Score}_{cv/pen}(M_G) - \text{Score}_{cv/pen}(M_A)$ must use a complexity-penalized or cross-validated metric (out-of-sample expected log predictive density, LOO-CV, WAIC, held-out log predictive density), never raw training fit, to exclude trivial overfitting of the interaction term.

Three sub-conditions make this operational. *Boundary-gated prediction and directional barrier-lowering*: $M_G$ must surpass $M_A$ by the pre-registered margin, and additionally the interaction must raise the transition probability *toward* the Lambda-like target, $P(S^I_{t+1}=\Lambda\text{-like}\mid u_t, \varepsilon^b_t, N^{op}_t) > P(S^I_{t+1}=\Lambda\text{-like}\mid u_t, N^{op}_t)$; if the interaction predicts delay, disruption, or stabilization away from the target, the result is boundary-gating but not barrier-lowering. *Orthogonal macro-invariant preservation* — the load-bearing condition — requires $D^{eff}_{id} < D_c$ assessed through a pre-specified orthogonal proxy $Z^I_t$ that does *not* share a measurement channel, frequency band, or generative source with the observable defining $S^I_{t+1}$, so that "the invariant was preserved" is not true by definition. On Path A, where the transition is declared by fast cortical signatures, $Z^I_t$ should be tracked through deep infra-slow subcortical-thalamic phase-coupling or intrinsic optical monitoring of structural membrane cross-linking; on Path C, where the transition monitors emergence or command-following, the invariant should be mapped by multiscale entropy or detrended-fluctuation-analysis scaling of the cardiac R-R series on a lagged independent window, or by heartbeat-evoked-potential morphology restricted to posterior-insula/mid-cingulate source components, mathematically independent of the frontal alpha-theta dynamics used to classify the state. The independence is methodological, not metaphysical: orthogonality of measurement is not ontological independence, and these proxies remain part of the classical substrate $C$. This concession must be sharpened, because a weaker reading of it would let the orthogonal-preservation test claim more than it can deliver. Measurement-channel separation — distinct sensors, frequency bands, generative-source localization, lagged windows — secures only a *statistical dissociation* of the proxy signal from the transition classifier. It does not secure *causal independence* of the two from a common underlying generator. This matters most acutely on Path C and during anesthetic emergence: cardiac and interoceptive variables are not autonomous of central dynamics, but are coupled to brainstem and insular activity, and the changes in heartbeat-evoked-potential morphology or R-R complexity that accompany emergence are in substantial part downstream consequences of the same ascending neuromodulatory reactivation that drives the cortical transition being classified. The proxy $Z^I_t$ and the classifier $S^I_{t+1}$ may therefore share a single classical generator — the collapse of the anesthetic state — even when they are measured through fully separated channels. The orthogonality requirement is not thereby vacuous, but its force must be stated exactly: it prevents the trivial circularity in which the invariant is "preserved" by definitional overlap with the transition variable, and nothing more. It does not, and is not claimed to, establish that the preserved invariant is causally independent of the transition. A positive orthogonal-preservation result is consequently evidence of *measurement-level* continuity of a declared macro-invariant through the transition, not evidence that this continuity is generated by anything other than the same dynamics producing the transition. Where the shared-generator structure is strong — as it is for cardiac proxies during emergence — even a clean statistical pass leaves open that the proxy and the classifier are two readouts of one process, and the result must be reported under that limitation rather than as causal corroboration. This bound has a sharp and damaging consequence for the preservation test that must be confronted directly: if the proxy $Z^I_t$ and the classifier $S^I_{t+1}$ are both driven by the ascending neuromodulatory generator (the brainstem noradrenergic surge), then the continuity of $Z^I_t$ through the transition is *physiologically trivial* — it records only that the common generator kept firing, that the brainstem did not die during anesthesia, not that an active boundary shielded an identity projection by non-coercive reconfiguration. A raw-signal preservation pass would therefore measure the survival of the generator, not the persistence of the invariant, and would bleed straight back into the operational null. To have any discriminating force, the preservation test cannot be run on the raw proxy. It must be run on the *deep orthogonal residual*: the continuity of $Z^I_t$ must be assessed after **conditional subtraction** of the variance explained by the common generator — that is, after regressing out the linear and nonlinear contribution of an estimated ascending-neuromodulatory/brainstem-activation signal (by phase regression against the estimated neuromodulatory drive, or by nonlinear Granger / transfer-entropy removal of the brainstem signal's predictive share), so that what is tested for persistence is the component of the invariant that survives *beyond* what the shared generator determines. Stated as a no-go: if the invariant's continuity disappears once the common-generator variance is conditioned out — if $Z^I_t$ is preserved only in the raw signal and not in the deep residual — then the Rung-5 pass was an artifact of the shared drive and the branch fails at that rung. Only persistence in the generator-subtracted residual counts, because only that residual can distinguish boundary-shielded continuity from the brainstem simply remaining alive.

This generator-subtracted-residual test was audited directly by simulation, and the audit sharpens the no-go into a quantitative condition that is more demanding than the prose above admits. A synthetic common generator $Z$ was made to drive both a boundary proxy and a macro-invariant, with a genuine second-order boundary-gating coupling present in one world and absent in a matched control; the residual interaction test was then run after regressing out a *realistically noisy proxy* of $Z$ (the only thing an experimenter has), with significance assessed against an autocorrelation-preserving block-permutation null. Three findings resulted. First, the worry that common-generator subtraction would destroy statistical power is *unfounded*: against a *linearly* acting generator the test recovered the genuine coupling with full power even at short records, while holding the false-positive rate at nominal — provided the null preserves the boundary gate's autocorrelation (a naive shuffle inflates false positives roughly threefold, which is why the autocorrelation-preserving surrogate the architecture already specifies in section 12 is mandatory here too). Second, and decisively, against a *nonlinearly* acting generator — one in which the shared drive itself carries an interaction term ($Z_t\times g_t$), the strong form of the shared-generator worry — the residual test *false-positives*, mistaking the nonlinear common generator for genuine boundary gating, and the false-positive rate *grows with record length* (reaching $\approx 0.9$ at the longest records tested), the unmistakable signature of a systematic confound rather than sampling noise. Third, this nonlinear-generator confound is governed by the *fidelity of the generator measurement* and follows an errors-in-variables threshold: when the measured proxy captures $\gtrsim 95\%$ of the generator's variance the false-positive rate returns to nominal, but as proxy fidelity degrades the false-positive rate climbs steeply ($\approx 0.1$ at $95\%$ fidelity, $\approx 0.3$ at $89\%$, $\approx 0.6$ at $75\%$). Crucially, attempting to fix this by also subtracting the proxy's *interaction* term does not work, because a noisy proxy of a nonlinear generator cannot be cleanly subtracted — regression dilution leaves the confound in the residual. The consequence for the architecture is exact and must be stated as a condition of validity, not buried: Rung 5 is statistically executable *only* when the common generator can be measured at high fidelity (proxy variance-capture $\gtrsim 95\%$); below that, a Rung-5 pass is increasingly likely to be a nonlinear-shared-generator artifact, and more data makes this worse, not better. In the architecture's own worked application — anesthetic emergence, where the common generator is a latent ascending-neuromodulatory cascade observed only through indirect and partial proxies — this fidelity condition is unlikely to be met, so a Rung-5 positive on Path C must be discounted accordingly and cannot, on current measurements, carry the load the architecture assigns it. This does not retract the rung; it bounds it, and the bound is tighter than "report under the standing possibility of a shared generator" — it is "the rung is informative only above a measurement-fidelity threshold that the flagship application probably cannot reach." Decisively, the invariant must be demonstrable *during* the transition; a macro-invariant that lapses during the transition and is re-established afterward is, by the standards of the companion framework, a reconstructed match rather than a preserved trajectory, and a result that cannot exclude this is not evidence for identity-relevant persistence regardless of how well it predicts the transition. *Structured adaptive openness*: $D^{eff}_{ag} > 0$ requires restored openness to external perturbation (sensory-evoked or TMS-EEG perturbational responsiveness), not mere autonomic maintenance, closed vegetative rhythms, internal reverberation, or uncoupled noise; cardiac or respiratory phase-locking are controls, not evidence of exteroceptive openness.

The interaction term is essential: if $u_t$ predicts $S^I_{t+1}$ independently of $\varepsilon^b_t$, the result is ordinary forcing; if $\varepsilon^b_t$ predicts independently of $u_t$, it is boundary contribution but not gating; if both are additive but non-interactive, it still does not instantiate biological Class G. This blocks the generic-shield objection: a boundary variable is not CBRA-relevant merely because it tracks cellular health, since generic survival should be absorbed by $N^{op}_t$. Predicting an *I*-supporting transition does not prove personal identity; it tests the biological precondition — whether the boundary architecture protects the functional projection required for the trajectory to resume at all.

## 9. Non-Substitutable Functions

CBRA does not require any particular molecule to be correct; it requires that four functions be performed by *some* architecture. *Directional boundary asymmetry* suppresses destructive thermal backaction while preserving forward transduction (absent: $Q_I$ thermalizes or goes inert). *Repair below threshold* corrects defects before they percolate into the macro-invariant (absent: dirty Lambda collapses into ordinary noisy attraction). *Preserved macro-invariant* maintains a memory/topological/correlation invariant not reducible to local relaxation (absent: CBRA is indistinguishable from classical attractor dynamics). *Causal transduction to $C$* produces measurable effects on excitability, channels, synchronization, or population dynamics (absent: the structure is epiphenomenal). Multiple realization is permitted only under constraint: a candidate mechanism may fail without collapsing CBRA provided some admissible realizer remains, but if *all* plausible mechanisms for one non-substitutable function are absent, the biological hypothesis fails.

## 10. Primary Implementation Paths

CBRA does not depend on a maximal conjunction of speculative mechanisms. The cytoskeletal-boundary block — Paths A and B — is sufficient for the central empirical program; the cardio-interoceptive Path C is a distinct branch, defended on its own terms.

### 10.1 Path A: Cytoskeletal Boundary and Anesthetic-State Transition

This path asks whether cytoskeletal or boundary-state residuals contribute independently to reversible consciousness-state transitions:

$$\text{Score}\big(M_1 : S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^b_t\big) > \text{Score}\big(M_0 : S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t\big),$$

with $b_t$ measured cytoskeletal boundary states and Score the declared out-of-sample metric. Anesthesia is attractive because it provides reversible transitions. This path is not the primary biological analogue of Class G — it tests boundary survival and dirty-Lambda persistence under reversible suppression and recovery, while Class G-like tests require boundary-gated perturbations that preserve adaptive degrees of freedom. CBRA should not deny receptor, presynaptic, mitochondrial, membrane, metabolic, active-inference, or attractor-dynamical mechanisms; it should ask whether cytoskeletal residuals explain transition structure *after* those are included (Mashour et al. 2020; Perouansky et al. 2023; Speigel et al. 2023; Curic et al. 2024). A positive pattern is out-of-sample improvement when structurally qualified residuals are added; a negative pattern is disappearance of predictive value after controls for receptor action, transmission, firing rate, metabolism, toxicity, hemodynamics, respiration, temperature, drug nonspecificity, and operational active-inference predictors.

### 10.2 Path B: Boundary-to-Membrane Transduction

This path asks whether boundary-conditioned molecular states can alter membrane excitability, channel probability, local field phase, or oscillatory synchronization, with minimum prediction $\Delta P(\text{channel open}\mid E_{Q_I})\neq 0$ within a biologically relevant parameter window. If the effect exists only under artificial overexpression, non-physiological illumination, extreme fields, or toxic perturbation, it is not CBRA evidence. The decisive issue is whether boundary variables alter drift terms, barrier heights, or transition probabilities under relevant conditions. Sub-molecular mechanisms (CISS, radical pairs, isotope branches) are not part of the minimum requirements and are suspended to Appendix A; Paths A and B define the minimum viable cytoskeletal-boundary test.

### 10.3 Path C: Cardio-Interoceptive Boundary as Receptivity Proxy

#### 10.3.1 The Heart-Transplant Constraint and the Retreat from Constitutive Status

The naive version of Path C made a claim that cannot survive a single decisive observation, and this paper retreats from it explicitly. The claim was that cardio-interoceptive variables ($h_t$ — HRV, HEP, baroreflex dynamics) are *constitutive* invariants of the psychological or historical self. The decisive observation against it is the heart transplant: a recipient whose biological heart — with its entire HRV signature, its baroreflex set-points, its heartbeat-evoked potential morphology — is surgically removed and replaced by a donor organ does *not* thereby become a different person. Autobiographical memory, relational history, responsibility-bearing agency, and narrative identity persist across the replacement. If the cardiac vector were a constitutive invariant, the transplant would be an identity-destroying event, which it is not. Call this the heart-transplant constraint: *no variable whose wholesale replacement leaves personal identity intact can be a constitutive invariant of that identity.* The cardiac vector fails this constraint, so it cannot be constitutive. This is not a refinement to be argued around; it is a refutation of the constitutive reading, and the reading is withdrawn.

The retreat, however, does not weaken the paper — it strengthens it, because once the constitutive claim is dropped, the transplant case *confirms* the framework rather than refuting it. The framework's central distinction is between constitutive and indicative/boundary structure, and the transplant is a clean demonstration of exactly that distinction operating in tissue. The self survives the mechanical replacement of the organ (constitutive invariant preserved), *and yet* the recipient's autonomic homeostasis, interoceptive texture, baroreflex dynamics, and the felt quality of bodily self-regulation change radically and must be re-anchored at real thermodynamic and adaptive cost. Both halves of this are predicted by reclassifying $h_t$ as boundary rather than constitutive: the constitutive trajectory is the kind of thing that survives organ replacement, while the boundary economy is the kind of thing whose replacement is survivable but *costly* and *re-anchoring*. The transplant thus instantiates the framework's own constitutive/boundary partition with unusual clarity. What would have refuted a claim the paper should never have made instead supports the claim the paper does make.

#### 10.3.2 Cardio-Interoceptive Variables as Receptivity-Boundary Proxies

The corrected status of $h_t$ is as a proxy for the *receptivity boundary* of biological Class G — the boundary structure $\chi(\theta_E)$ whose state gates the efficacy of an external perturbation — not as a proxy for the constitutive invariant $Q_I$. Cardiac rhythm, vagal signaling, baroreflex dynamics, respiratory sinus arrhythmia, heartbeat-evoked potentials, and cardiorespiratory coupling form part of the embodied boundary economy through which arousal, attention, affective salience, and adaptive openness are regulated, via the visceral and interoceptive pathways through which bodily signals reach cortical processing (Critchley and Harrison 2013; Craig 2009; Thayer and Lane 2009; Seth and Tsakiris 2018; Park and Blanke 2019). On this reading, $h_t$ indexes the orientation/receptivity arm of the boundary, and its residual is tested for whether it gates the efficacy of an intervention, not for whether it carries identity.

This reclassification justifies Path C as a distinct branch on three structural grounds that the cytoskeletal path cannot supply. First, the cardio-interoceptive system is the one place where the active boundary is *spatially distributed across organs*: baroreflex loops, vagal afferents, and heartbeat-evoked cortical responses constitute a boundary whose sensing and active arms are realized in cardiac and autonomic tissue while the protected classical substrate $C$ is neural. This is the only branch that operationalizes the claim that the boundary economy is "not borne by neural tissue alone." Second, healthy cardiac dynamics already exhibit the *dirty-Lambda signature* — structured multiscale variability that is neither Class P metronomy nor Class M dispersal, with well-developed measures (multiscale entropy, detrended fluctuation analysis, complexity loss in pathology) — so Path C has an *independently characterizable* invariant proxy (cardiac complexity structure) not derived from the observable used to declare the transition, which is exactly what the load-bearing orthogonal-preservation condition requires. This advantage must be stated with the qualification of section 8.1: "not derived from the observable" means not sharing a measurement channel with the transition classifier, which is a statistical dissociation, not a guarantee of causal independence from the shared central generator (see below). Third, interventions that modulate the cardio-interoceptive boundary — paced breathing, vagal stimulation, baroreflex manipulation, interoceptive attention — are non-invasive, reversible, and dose-controllable in humans, making the boundary-gating interaction term $u_t\times\varepsilon^h_t$ empirically tractable years before it is tractable cytoskeletally.

The vector and residual:

$$h_t = [HRV_t,\ BRS_t,\ CP_t,\ RSA_t,\ HEP_t,\ CRP_t]^\top, \qquad \varepsilon^h_t = h_t - \widehat{h}_t(N^{op}_t),$$

with the minimal prediction $\text{Score}(M_1 : S^I_{t+1}\sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^h_t) > \text{Score}(M_0 : S^I_{t+1}\sim N^{op}_t)$. Candidate outcomes include sleep-wake transition, emergence, recovery of responsiveness, stabilization of attention, recovery from dissociation, and affective recovery after stress. A positive result requires $\varepsilon^h_t$ to improve out-of-sample prediction after controls for respiration, hemodynamics, medication, motor artifact, age, cardiovascular fitness, arousal, metabolic state, and neural variables; a negative result occurs if cardiac variables are absorbed by $N^{op}_t$ or attributable to generic physiology. Path C is therefore conservative: it tests whether embodied persistence is partly maintained by a trans-organ receptivity boundary rather than by neural tissue alone — and it never treats the heart as a cognitive organ.

One limitation specific to Path C must be recorded explicitly rather than left to the general caveat of section 8.1, because it is sharpest precisely here. The cardio-interoceptive proxies proposed for the orthogonal-preservation test — R-R complexity, heartbeat-evoked-potential morphology — are not causally autonomous of the central transition they are meant to be checked against. Cardiac and interoceptive dynamics are coupled to brainstem and insular activity, and during anesthetic emergence their changes are in large part downstream of the same ascending neuromodulatory reactivation that produces the cortical state change used to classify the transition. The measurement orthogonality the protocol demands (separate sensors, bands, windows, source-localized components) is therefore real but limited: it is a statistical dissociation that defeats definitional circularity, not a demonstration that the cardiac proxy and the neural classifier are driven by different processes. In the strong-coupling regime of emergence they may well be two readouts of one generator. Path C accordingly does not claim that a clean orthogonal-preservation pass on the raw signal establishes a causally independent cardiac invariant; it requires that preservation be demonstrated in the *generator-subtracted residual* (section 8.1) — the component of the cardiac invariant that survives after the estimated ascending-neuromodulatory/brainstem drive has been conditionally regressed out — and it reports any positive result under the standing possibility of a shared central generator. This does not collapse the branch — measurement-level continuity in the deep residual through transition remains a non-trivial and rejectable test — but it bounds what a pass can mean, and that bound, together with the conditional-subtraction requirement, is part of the branch's pre-registered interpretation, not an afterthought.

## 11. Experimental Design Requirements

A CBRA-positive experiment specifies the following before data collection.

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Target transition | Define $S^I_{t+1}$ in advance: induction, emergence, sleep-wake transition, recovery, availability, or population shift. |
| Operational baseline | Specify $N^{op}_t$: receptor occupancy, dose, firing rate, synaptic variables, metabolism, excitability, EEG/MEG features, effective connectivity, network state, hemodynamics, respiratory phase, temperature, operational active-inference/Markov-blanket proxies. |
| Boundary-state measure | Specify $b_t$ independently: cytoskeletal stability/polymerization and membrane-cytoskeleton coupling, or another physically measured boundary-state pair. |
| Residualization | Define $\varepsilon^b_t = b_t - \widehat{b}_t(N^{op}_t)$ with a pre-specified model avoiding both under-control and destructive overfitting. |
| Structural qualification | Require temporal persistence, repair-threshold behavior, slow-wave-confound resistance, mechanistic interpretability, prospective predictive value — *and* the three tests of section 12. |
| Control conditions | Toxicity, metabolic stress, drug nonspecificity, motor impairment, inflammation, respiration, hemodynamics, temperature drift, measurement drift, ordinary network dynamics. |
| Model comparison | Compare $M_0$ against $M_1$ out of sample. |
| Failure criterion | Declare when $\varepsilon^b_t$ is redundant, epiphenomenal, non-generalizing, irrelevant, unmeasurable, or absorbed. |
| Cardio-interoceptive vector | Specify $h_t$ in advance: HRV, BRS, cardiac phase, RSA, HEP, CRP, with acquisition channel and preprocessing pipeline. |
| Cardiac confound controls | Respiration, hemodynamic drift, medication, arousal, age, fitness, posture, movement, sleep stage, metabolic state, cardiac pathology before treating $\varepsilon^h_t$ as independent. |
| HEP artifact control | Demonstrate HEP effects are not ECG contamination, pulse artifact, or circular use of the EEG feature defining the transition. |

Without this operational layer CBRA would be only a vocabulary for redescribing neural complexity. With it, CBRA becomes a risky architecture: it can be rejected.

### 11.1 Boundary-State Variables and Structural Qualification

The measured boundary-state vector is $b_t = [MPS_t, MCC_t]^\top$, with $MPS_t$ microtubule polymerization/stability and $MCC_t$ membrane-cytoskeleton coupling — physical state variables, with residual $\varepsilon^b_t = b_t - \widehat{b}_t(N^{op}_t)$. $N^{op}_t$ is the measured operational predictor set, not a theoretical framework. The residual counts as CBRA-relevant only if it passes structural qualification: persistent temporal structure rather than white noise; repair-threshold behavior ($\delta < \delta_c \Rightarrow$ recovery, $\delta > \delta_c \Rightarrow$ collapse or transition); survival of controls for generic slow oscillations; mechanistic tie to boundary repair or membrane-cytoskeleton transduction; prospective improvement of $S^I_{t+1}$ beyond $N^{op}_t$. A residual that fails these is unmodeled physiology, measurement noise, or an artifact of an underspecified null — not partial evidence.

## 12. The Three-Test Residual Qualification Protocol

The single most serious objection a neuroscientific reviewer can raise is that any structured boundary residual is merely *long-tailed stochastic fluctuation of background neural noise* — that the apparent structure is a heavy-tailed but ultimately linear-stochastic artifact, not the signature of an organizationally maintained invariant. The qualification criteria of sections 3.3 and 11.1 are necessary but not sufficient to defeat this objection, because a sufficiently heavy-tailed linear process can mimic persistence and prospective prediction over finite windows. This section adds three explicit tests, applied to the post-transition residual series, each targeting a distinct way the stochastic-fluctuation null could survive the earlier filters. All three must be pre-registered, and all three must pass.

### 12.1 Test One: Relaxation Asymmetry

A linear stochastic surge — a large excursion of an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck-like background process — is, by construction, *mean-reverting*: after the excursion, the process relaxes back toward its prior attractor, and its mean and variance return to baseline. The signature of an organizationally maintained transition is the opposite. Test One requires that at $t_0^+$ the residual undergo a *permanent structural break* in its mean and/or variance — a step change that does **not** relax back to the pre-transition attractor over the relevant window. Operationally, this is a change-point test (for example, a pre-registered cumulative-sum or Bayesian change-point analysis) on the residual's first two moments, with the additional requirement that the post-break level be stable rather than transient: the residual must establish and hold a new statistical regime rather than excursion-and-return. A residual that spikes and relaxes is consistent with the stochastic-surge null and *fails* Test One; only a residual that breaks and *stays broken* — failing to return to the old attractor — eliminates the linear mean-reverting hypothesis. The conceptual content is that conversion of regime, not deviation within a regime, is what a maintained transition produces, and mean-reversion is the diagnostic of mere deviation.

### 12.2 Test Two: Idempotent Covariance Rotation

A heavy-tailed background fluctuation, however large, preserves the *geometry* of the local dynamics: the eigenvectors of the local covariance matrix $\mathbf{\Sigma}_\varepsilon(t)$ — the principal axes along which the residual varies — are not reorganized by a stochastic excursion; their orientation is a property of the underlying linear operator, which a surge does not change. The signature of an imposed organizational constraint is the opposite: an *instantaneous orthogonal rotation* of the eigenvectors of $\mathbf{\Sigma}_\varepsilon(t)$ at $t_0^+$. Test Two tracks the local covariance matrix through the transition and requires that its eigenvector frame undergo a step rotation — a discrete reorientation of the principal axes — at the transition point. The rotation being *orthogonal* (preserving the dimensionality and the inner-product structure while reorienting the frame) and *idempotent in effect* (the new frame is a stable projection, not a continuously drifting one) is the signature that dynamical pathways have been *geometrically closed* — that certain previously available directions of variation are now foreclosed. This is the statistical fingerprint of the imposition of a **non-holonomic constraint**: a constraint on the accessible directions of motion that cannot be integrated into a simple restriction on position, manifesting precisely as a reorganization of which directions the local dynamics may explore. A stochastic surge produces no such rotation; the eigenframe is invariant under it. A residual whose covariance eigenframe rotates orthogonally and stably at $t_0^+$ therefore exhibits geometric pathway-closure that the fluctuation null cannot produce, and a residual whose eigenframe is invariant through the transition *fails* Test Two.

### 12.3 Test Three: Multivariate IAAFT Surrogate Testing

The third test confronts the stochastic-fluctuation null directly and at scale, using the strongest available surrogate-data method for the multivariate, nonlinear case (Theiler et al. 1992; Schreiber and Schmitz 1996; Kantz and Schreiber 2004). The post-transition residual series is subjected to multivariate **IAAFT** (iterative amplitude-adjusted Fourier transform) surrogate generation: an ensemble of 10,000 surrogates is produced, each of which preserves, to high accuracy, the *exact linear properties* of the real multivariate series — its amplitude distribution and its full cross-power-spectrum (hence all linear auto- and cross-correlations) — while destroying nonlinear structure and high-order phase dependence by randomizing Fourier phases. The surrogates are, by construction, the best possible linear-stochastic mimics of the real residual: anything that distinguishes the real series from the surrogate ensemble is, by the logic of surrogate testing, nonlinear and not reducible to linear stochastic fluctuation. Test Three requires that a pre-registered discriminating statistic — a measure of nonlinear complexity and high-order phase dependence, such as a multivariate nonlinear-prediction error, a higher-order phase-coupling index, or a transfer-entropy-based statistic — computed on the real residual fall *outside the 99.9% confidence interval* of the same statistic computed across the 10,000 surrogates. Passing Test Three rejects, at the 99.9% level, the null hypothesis that the residual's structure is a linear stochastic fluctuation of background neural noise: the real series is shown to carry nonlinear complexity and high-order phase structure that no linear surrogate with identical spectrum and amplitude distribution can reproduce. A residual that lies within the surrogate confidence interval is statistically indistinguishable from a linear stochastic process with the same spectrum and *fails* Test Three; it must be treated as background fluctuation regardless of how well it predicts the transition.

The three tests are complementary and jointly necessary. Test One rules out mean-reverting excursion (relaxation back to the old attractor). Test Two rules out geometry-preserving fluctuation (no eigenframe reorganization). Test Three rules out spectrum-matched linear stochasticity (no nonlinear high-order structure). A residual that passes all three has been shown to (i) establish a new and persistent statistical regime, (ii) reorganize the geometry of accessible dynamical directions, and (iii) carry nonlinear complexity beyond any linear surrogate.

What this does and does not exclude must be stated with precision, because an inflated reading of the three tests would be fatal to the protocol's honesty. The tests exclude *linear stochastic fluctuation of background noise* — and only that. They do not exclude nonlinear deterministic or chaotic classical dynamics, and it is essential to concede this outright: a low-dimensional deterministic chaotic system such as the Lorenz attractor, or a trivial nonlinear neural network with delayed feedback loops, will reject the IAAFT surrogate null at $p < 0.001$; a system under a passive mechanical non-holonomic constraint will exhibit the covariance-eigenframe reorganization of Test Two; and a system undergoing a genuine regime change for ordinary reasons will show the structural break of Test One. Passing all three therefore does *not* signal the presence of an organizationally maintained identity-bearing boundary. It signals only that the residual is nonlinear, possibly deterministic or chaotic, and subject to geometric constraint — properties that are entirely compatible with, and indeed expected of, ordinary nonlinear brain and cardiac dynamics under the standard paradigm of nonlinear biological physics. As an *eliminative* instrument the three-test protocol is consequently far weaker than its statistical sophistication suggests: it kills the linear-noise null and nothing else.

The discriminating burden therefore does not rest on the three tests at all. The three tests are a necessary *gate* — a residual that fails them is background noise and is discarded — but they are nowhere near sufficient, because the live competitor is never linear noise alone; it is a common nonlinear classical generator (section 8.1's shared-generator problem, sharpest on Path C during emergence: the same ascending neuromodulatory cascade driving both the cortical classifier and the cardiac proxy). Discriminating CBRA from such a generator is the work of the conditions the three tests do not perform: prospective non-redundant prediction against a null that *itself includes* nonlinear and attractor-dynamical predictors (section 3), the orthogonal macro-invariant-preservation test with its measurement-versus-causal caveat (section 8.1), the boundary-gating interaction term that ordinary forcing cannot produce (section 8.1), and perturbational tractability and generalization (section 3.3). Even the conjunction of all of these does not cleanly separate an actively maintained boundary from a sufficiently rich multiscale nonlinear homeostatic reactivation, and the architecture concedes that this separation may not be achievable with current measurements at all — which is precisely why CBRA is conditional and is built to return a negative. Passing the three tests, then, is a necessary condition for treating the residual as provisional evidence, conjoined with every later requirement; it is never, on its own, a demonstration of anything more than that the residual is not linear noise — and the gap between "not linear noise" and "an identity-bearing boundary rather than a common nonlinear generator" is exactly the gap the rest of the protocol, and ultimately the limits of measurement, must try and may fail to close.

The protocol's role is therefore defensive: the three tests are an *exclusion filter against trivial physical criticality*, not a detector of an identity-bearing boundary. Many ordinary systems sit near critical points, undergo regime changes, and carry nonlinear structure; a great deal of biology is generically critical. The filter's job is to discard residuals whose structure is exhausted by linear stochasticity — the cheapest and most common false positive — so that scarce analytic and inferential effort is spent only on residuals that are at least nonlinear, regime-changing, and geometrically constrained. Nothing about passing the filter promotes a residual past that gate; the contract $I$, pre-specified before any boundary variable is inspected, and the out-of-sample predictive comparison against the strong, strictly nonlinear operational null remain the only instruments that can do the promoting. It is possible to re-read each test in the vocabulary of complex-systems physics — Test One as a temporal-symmetry break in entropy-production rate across a bifurcation, Test Two as the emergence of a macroscopic order parameter that reorganizes the covariance eigenframe as local precision is reallocated, Test Three as the signature of nonlinear mean-field dynamics carrying the system between distinct invariant manifolds — and such re-readings can be useful for situating the tests within nonlinear biophysics. But they are interpretive analogies, not additional evidence: a system can exhibit every one of these complex-systems signatures and still be an ordinary nonlinear generator with no identity-bearing boundary whatever. The re-readings change how the filter is described; they do not change what passing it licenses, which remains, strictly, "not linear noise" and nothing more.

**The leading non-boundary competitor, named explicitly: self-organized criticality.** The generic alternative the three tests cannot exclude is not a vague "nonlinear generator" but a specific and well-evidenced one that deserves to be named as the protocol's principal rival, because the cortex is widely argued to operate near a critical point. Neuronal-avalanche dynamics and self-organized criticality (SOC) generically produce *exactly* the three signatures the tests detect: scale-free avalanches give heavy-tailed, nonlinear structure that rejects the IAAFT linear-stochastic null (Test Three); a phase transition through or away from the critical point produces a persistent change in the variance regime (Test One); and the reorganization of the dominant collective modes at a critical transition reorganizes the covariance eigenframe (Test Two). A system poised at or moving through self-organized criticality therefore passes all three tests *with no identity-bearing boundary anywhere in it*. This is not a hypothetical worry: criticality is one of the most actively defended accounts of resting and transitional cortical dynamics, and anesthetic state transitions in particular have been modeled as movements toward or away from a critical point. The consequence for CBRA is stark and must be stated rather than buried: a structured boundary residual that survives the three tests is, on current evidence, *more parsimoniously* explained by ordinary cortical criticality than by a maintained identity-bearing boundary, and the burden is entirely on the later stages of the protocol — prospective non-redundant prediction against a null that *itself contains criticality-derived predictors* (avalanche statistics, branching-parameter estimates, distance-to-criticality measures), the boundary-gating interaction term, and generalization across perturbation classes — to separate the two. The architecture concedes that this separation may not be achievable: if every structured residual CBRA can detect is reproducible by a critical cortical generator, then CBRA has no empirical content beyond criticality, and that is one of the principal ways the whole program fails. The honest position is that the criticality competitor is not a confound to be controlled away but a rival hypothesis of equal or greater prior plausibility, and CBRA earns a hearing only if a boundary residual predicts an $I$-supporting transition *after a criticality-complete null has been included*, which is a far heavier burden than the three tests impose.

## 13. Validation Ladder and No-Go Commitments

CBRA proceeds up a ladder, with a no-go commitment at each rung; failure at a rung halts ascent for that branch.

1. **Measurability.** The boundary vector must be measurable with resolution sufficient to remain independent of $N^{op}_t$. *No-go:* if it cannot, the branch has no empirical foothold.
2. **Independence.** The residual must satisfy the four independence conditions of section 4. *No-go:* if it is a disguised $N^{op}_t$ measure, stop.
3. **Structural qualification.** The residual must pass section 11.1's criteria and the three tests of section 12. *No-go:* if it is background fluctuation, stop. Note that passing the three tests excludes only linear stochastic noise; it does *not* clear the self-organized-criticality competitor (section 12.3), which is deferred to rung 4's null.
4. **Prospective non-redundant prediction.** $M_1$ must beat $M_0$ out of sample, where $N^{op}_t$ includes criticality-derived predictors (avalanche statistics, branching parameter, distance-to-criticality). *No-go:* if absorbed — including absorbed by the criticality predictors — the branch fails.
5. **Orthogonal invariant preservation through the transition.** An independent proxy must show the macro-invariant preserved *during* the transition, and — where proxy and classifier may share a common generator — preserved in the *generator-subtracted residual*, after the shared brainstem/neuromodulatory drive has been conditionally regressed out (section 8.1). *No-go:* if the invariant lapses and is reconstructed, or if its continuity survives only in the raw signal and vanishes once the common-generator variance is conditioned out, the result is a reconstructed match or a shared-drive artifact, not persistence — the strongest single failure. *Measurement-fidelity precondition (established by the section 8.1 audit):* because a noisy proxy of a *nonlinearly* acting common generator cannot be cleanly subtracted, this rung is informative only when the common generator is measured at high fidelity (proxy variance-capture $\gtrsim 95\%$); below that threshold the residual test false-positives on a nonlinear shared generator at a rate that grows with record length, so a pass is uninformative — and on Path C during anesthetic emergence, where the generator is a latent neuromodulatory cascade, this precondition is unlikely to be met.
5b. **Identity-linkage dissociation.** The residual's predictive and invariant-preserving structure must be *conditional on the $I$-relevance of the transition*: present for $I$-preserving transitions and absent for surface-matched $I$-breaching ones (section 14, $M_{\text{diss}}$). *No-go:* a residual that predicts $I$-preserving and matched $I$-breaching transitions alike is a generic transition marker, not an $I$-indexed channel — CBRA-positive for "structure exists," CBRA-negative for identity-linkage. This rung is what separates the architecture from a search for an unexplained gap; where matched $I$-breaching transitions are ethically or practically unobtainable, the rung returns "undetermined" and the identity-linkage claim is withheld rather than asserted on a main effect alone.
6. **Boundary-gated transformation.** The interaction $u_t\times\varepsilon^b_t$ must improve prediction directionally toward the target. *No-go:* additive-only contribution does not instantiate biological Class G.
7. **Generalization.** Effects must hold across preparations, subjects, perturbation classes, or platforms. *No-go:* parochial effects are not architectural.

The decisive sub-molecular branches (Appendix A) are explicitly outside this ladder's load-bearing core; the ladder is climbable, and falsifiable, on the cytoskeletal and cardio-interoceptive routes alone.

## 14. Experimental Prediction Families

**Classical model comparison.** The decisive test is nested comparison of $M_0 : S^I_{t+1}\sim N^{op}_t$ against $M_1 : S^I_{t+1}\sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^b_t$. Positive evidence requires $M_1$ to improve prediction, generalize across datasets, and survive controls for toxicity, metabolism, firing rate, pharmacology, synaptic variables, hemodynamic drift, respiratory phase, and regularized slow-wave structure. If $\varepsilon^b_t$ adds no independent value after structural qualification (including section 12), CBRA fails.

**Cardio-interoceptive residual prediction.** $\varepsilon^h_t$ should improve out-of-sample prediction of $I$-supporting transitions beyond the null — emergence, sleep-wake transition, recovery of responsiveness, stabilization of attention, affective recovery, restoration of bodily presence after dissociation. A negative result occurs if HRV, BRS, cardiac phase, RSA, HEP, or CRP add no prospective value once respiratory, hemodynamic, pharmacological, metabolic, neural, and arousal variables are included.

**Boundary-gated cardio-interoceptive transformation.** If a cardio-interoceptive analogue of Class G is present, the effect of an external perturbation $u_t$ — breathing practice, vagal stimulation, neurofeedback, contemplative attention, sensory intervention, pharmacological modulation — should depend on the structured residual:

$$\begin{aligned} M_A &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + u_t + \varepsilon^h_t, \\ M_G &: S^I_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + u_t + \varepsilon^h_t + (u_t \times \varepsilon^h_t). \end{aligned}$$

A positive result requires $M_G$ to improve out-of-sample prediction over $M_A$, the effect to be directionally tied to the Lambda-like target, and adaptive openness to be preserved rather than suppressed. If the intervention merely sedates, immobilizes, or regularizes, it is damping, not Class G.

A methodological correction enforced by the proof of concept of section 15.5 must be applied to this comparison. The interaction-term improvement $M_G > M_A$, when measured as an AUC gain from adding the regressor $u_t\times\varepsilon^h_t$ to a flexible discriminative classifier, is *absorbable* by a sufficiently rich additive null when the boundary is strongly coupled to the central generator — it is not, on its own, a reliable signature of genuine gating, and a result resting on it alone is invalid. The load-bearing quantity is instead the *stratified conditional-success differential under a matched non-gating control*: the difference between the success rate of perturbations delivered while the boundary residual is in a receptive state and the rate while it is not, evaluated prospectively, *and contrasted against a placebo condition in which the same perturbation is delivered without timing to boundary state*. A genuine gating effect appears as a boundary-state-dependent perturbation efficacy that the placebo contrast lacks; an additive generative process cannot produce this differential. A bare nonlinear critical generator, however, *can* reproduce it under a clean read-out and weakly homeostatic feedback at realistic correlation (section 15.5), so the differential survives an additive criticality-complete null that absorbs the interaction coefficient but is not by itself diagnostic of gating — the directional barrier-lowering and cross-preparation generalization rungs must carry the discrimination. The interaction-model comparison $M_A$ versus $M_G$ is retained only as corroborating evidence subordinate to this differential.

### 14.1 The Identity-Linkage Dissociation

**The test that separates an $I$-channel from a generic surviving residual.** The preceding families share a structural blind spot that must be named as the most serious internal threat to CBRA and answered with a dedicated design, because without it a maximally successful CBRA result establishes far less than it appears to. Suppose every rung of section 13 is passed: a structurally qualified, independently measured, criticality-resistant boundary residual $\varepsilon^b_t$ prospectively predicts $S^I_{t+1}$ out of sample, and an orthogonal proxy shows the macro-invariant preserved through the transition. This still does not distinguish the hypothesis CBRA cares about — that $\varepsilon^b_t$ indexes a *persistence channel tied to the $I$-constitutive invariant* — from the far more probable and far more banal hypothesis that $\varepsilon^b_t$ indexes *some* structured physiological process the operational null happened not to contain (a slow neuromodulatory cascade, an unmapped glial or vascular dynamic, an omitted nonlinear interaction). Both hypotheses predict a surviving residual that forecasts transitions; survival against a null is common to them. The eliminative machinery of sections 3–13 is powerful against *unstructured* alternatives (noise, drift, linear confounds) and against *one named* structured alternative (criticality), but it is silent on the open-ended class "any other structured physiology," and a protocol that cannot address that class is, in the precise sense of the companion critique, a search for a gap rather than a test of a hypothesis. This is the God-of-the-gaps failure mode transposed into statistics, and CBRA does not escape it merely by being eliminative; it escapes it only by adding a *dissociation* that the identity-channel hypothesis predicts and the generic-residual hypothesis does not.

The design is a double dissociation across the $I$-relevance of the transition, holding the surface dynamics of the transition as nearly matched as the preparation allows. Partition target transitions into two classes *defined by the contract $I$, locked before data inspection*: $I$-preserving transitions $S^{I+}$ (induction and emergence under an agent expected to resume the same identity-bearing trajectory — reversible anesthesia, physiological sleep-wake) and $I$-breaching transitions $S^{I-}$ (transitions across which the $I$-constitutive invariant is, by the contract, *not* required or expected to be preserved — deep hypothermic circulatory arrest without recovery of the prior trajectory, transitions into states the contract classifies as replacement rather than continuation, or an experimentally matched non-recovering control where available and ethically permissible). The identity-channel hypothesis makes a *differential* prediction that no generic-residual hypothesis makes: $\varepsilon^b_t$ should carry predictive and invariant-preserving structure *selectively* for $S^{I+}$ and should *not* do so for $S^{I-}$ matched on arousal trajectory, metabolic depth, and gross EEG state. A generic structured physiological process, by contrast, tracks the *physical* transition and should predict $S^{I+}$ and $S^{I-}$ alike whenever their surface dynamics are matched, precisely because it is indifferent to a contract it knows nothing about. Formally, the load-bearing quantity is the interaction between the boundary residual and the $I$-class of the transition,

$$M_{\text{diss}} : S_{t+1} \sim N^{op}_t + \varepsilon^b_t + \mathbb{1}[I\text{-preserving}] + \big(\varepsilon^b_t \times \mathbb{1}[I\text{-preserving}]\big),$$

and CBRA-positive evidence for *identity-linkage specifically* requires the interaction coefficient to be non-zero out of sample after the main effects — a residual whose predictive validity is *conditional on the $I$-relevance of the transition*. A non-zero main effect for $\varepsilon^b_t$ with a null interaction is the signature of a generic transition marker and is CBRA-negative for the identity claim even though it is CBRA-positive for "a structured residual exists," and the two must never again be conflated after this test is available.

Three honesty conditions bound the dissociation and are stated so it is not over-sold. First, it is *ethically and practically hard*: genuine $I$-breaching transitions with matched surface dynamics are rare, often terminal, and frequently unobtainable in humans, so the design will usually be run on animal preparations or on naturally occurring matched pairs of unequal $I$-status, with all the external-validity costs that implies. Second, the matching is the whole game: an unmatched $S^{I-}$ (deeper, more metabolically extreme, differently shaped) reintroduces exactly the confound the dissociation was meant to remove, so the arousal, depth, and gross-state matching must be pre-registered and audited, and a pair that cannot be matched yields "undetermined," not a result. Third — and this is the deepest limit — the dissociation tests linkage to the *contract $I$*, not to personal identity, consciousness, or the soul; it shows at most that a boundary residual tracks the specific macro-invariant $I$ declares constitutive rather than tracking transitions generically, and the inference from "$I$-constitutive-invariant-linked" to any richer notion of the person remains governed by section 1.2 and is not advanced by this test. Fourth — and this bound is imposed by direct in-silico test rather than by prior reasoning (section 14.2) — the dissociation inherits the *criticality confound* that section 15.5 identifies for the gating differential: a bare critical generator with no identity mechanism whatever reproduces a growing share of the dissociation signature as the boundary process approaches criticality, reaching roughly $73\%$ of the identity-linked reference at the strongest, cleanest cell tested. The dissociation is therefore diagnostic of identity-linkage *only if the boundary process can be independently shown to be measurably sub-critical* (avalanche statistics, branching parameter, and distance-to-criticality bounded away from the critical point), a control CBRA does not otherwise require and which must be added to the design; absent it, a near-critical generic process can manufacture the very dissociation the test reads as identity-specific. This control carries a *measurement* requirement that direct in-silico test (section 14.2) shows is not optional but load-bearing: the sub-criticality must be established with a **subsampling-robust estimator** — a multistep-regression branching-ratio estimator of the Wilting–Priesemann (2018) type, or a clean event-segmentation of avalanches — and *not* the naive lag-one autoregressive slope, which fails in exactly the dangerous direction on the physiological observable. On a short, continuous, spatially subsampled stream the naive slope is attenuated toward zero, reading a near-critical $\sigma\approx 0.94$ as $\hat\sigma\approx 0.79$ and thereby false-certifying the majority of confounded near-critical systems as *safe* — the worst possible failure for a safety control, since it errs toward passing the very systems it exists to reject. A subsampling-robust estimator removes that attenuation and restores the control, but even then the control *compresses* the confound rather than eliminating it and admits a residual leak, so it is a necessary and now-executable guard, not a guarantee; the quantities are given in section 14.2. The estimator specification is thus part of the control itself: "measure sub-criticality" without "with a subsampling-robust estimator" is not merely incomplete but actively unsafe. With those bounds, the dissociation converts CBRA from a protocol that can only ever report "a residual survived" into one that can, at least in principle, report "a residual survived *and behaved as an identity-indexed channel would, not as a generic physiological process would*" — which is the only form of positive result that answers the gap objection rather than instantiating it.

### 14.2 In-Silico Feasibility Checks

Because CBRA analyzes no data, the only thing simulation can establish is whether the *statistical instrument* is sound — its power, its false-positive behavior, and its robustness to the confounds the architecture already names — before any subject is enrolled. A pre-registered suite tested exactly this: several of its results tighten the architecture's admitted limits into numbers, one motivates the sub-criticality control stated just above, and one tests whether that control can actually be run on a physiological signal. (Runs, pre-registrations, and raw metrics are archived and available; none of this is evidence about biological tissue, only about the test.)

The **criticality confound of the gating differential** (section 15.5) was quantified rather than left as a concession. A generator that is genuinely critical but contains no identity-bearing boundary reproduces the stratified conditional-success differential in $15$ of $24$ parameter cells ($62\%$), robustly for susceptibility exponents $p\ge 1.5$ across read-out noise and homeostatic feedback, with a magnitude that *matches or exceeds* the gated reference as criticality strengthens; the non-critical control ($p=1$) returns a differential of $\approx 0$, confirming the statistic itself is valid. This is a direct confirmation of section 15.5's retreat: the positive/detection arm is confounded by ordinary criticality across most of the parameter space, and CBRA's defensible arm is eliminative. The **dissociation test** of section 14.1 was then subjected to the same probe and found *more robust but not immune*: it is not confounded away from criticality (only $3$ of $12$ cells cross the $50\%$ threshold, versus $62\%$ for gating), but a bare critical generator reaches $\approx 73\%$ of the identity-linked reference at the strongest, lowest-noise cell, rising monotonically toward the critical point — which is the empirical basis for the fourth honesty condition above.

The **sub-criticality control itself was then put to direct test**: does requiring measured sub-criticality actually recover the dissociation's power against the pure critical confound, and can it be run on the kind of signal a real experiment would obtain? The control *concept* is sound. With the true distance-to-criticality known (an oracle), demanding measured sub-criticality drops the residual confound from a no-control mean of $\approx 38\%$ (worst cell $\approx 70\%$) of the identity-linked reference to $\approx 27\%$, and on cleanly separated avalanches a standard branching-ratio estimator reaches $\approx 24\%$ with near-perfect discrimination. But the physiological observable is neither an oracle nor a clean avalanche catalogue: it is a single short, continuous, spatially *subsampled* stream, and on it the **naive lag-one branching-ratio slope is attenuated in the dangerous direction** — a true $\sigma=0.94$ is read as $\hat\sigma\approx 0.79$, so the control false-certifies $\approx 79\%$ of confounded near-critical systems as safe, its certification power collapses to $\approx 0.21$, and the residual confound stays at $\approx 35\%$. This is the known subsampling bias (Wilting & Priesemann 2018), and it is the worst possible failure mode for a safety control because it errs toward passing exactly the systems the control exists to reject. The **subsampling-robust multistep-regression (MR) estimator** on the *same* short trace removes the attenuation ($\sigma=0.94 \to \hat\sigma_{\mathrm{MR}}\approx 0.92$) and restores the control: it accepts $\approx 90\%$ of genuinely sub-critical systems, rejects $\approx 83\%$ of confounded ones (certification power $\approx 0.75$), and compresses the residual confound to $\approx 29\%$. Three honest bounds attach even in this best realistic case, and the design must state them: the confound is *compressed, not eliminated* — its floor is $\approx 27\%$ of the reference even under the oracle, so a pure critical generator always leaves some dissociation-like residue; the control *leaks*, admitting $\approx 17\%$ of confounded near-critical systems as safe; and it *costs*, rejecting $\approx 10\%$ of genuinely sub-critical systems. The operative conclusion for the design is therefore precise: the fourth honesty condition's sub-criticality control is executable and recovers most of the dissociation's power, but *only* when distance-to-criticality is measured with a subsampling-robust estimator (MR-type) or from cleanly segmented avalanches — never the naive slope — and even then it is a leak-bounded guard, not an elimination of the criticality confound.

The **feasibility of the dissociation as an experiment** was mapped over sample size, effect size, and matching quality. The headline is that imperfect matching between the $S^{I+}$ and $S^{I-}$ conditions is a *validity* problem, not merely a power one: it inflates the false-positive rate from $0.05$ at perfect matching to $0.16$ and $0.35$ as the matching standard deviation grows to $0.3\sigma$ and $0.6\sigma$, *independent of sample size* — an unmatched contrast manufactures dissociations that are not there. Valid use therefore requires tight, independently verified matching (sd $\lesssim 0.3\sigma$), and even then a moderate effect (SNR $0.8$) needs $n\approx 40$ per condition for $80\%$ power, with small effects (SNR $\le 0.5$) out of reach at realistic sizes. This is a practical constraint that belongs in the design alongside the ethical one: the dissociation is executable but demands tight matching and substantial samples.

Finally, the **thermodynamic objection of section 7.2** — that a sufficiently spatially-resolved metabolic-configurational null would absorb the boundary residual — was given a resolution threshold. In a spectral energy-diffusion model the null absorbs the structured residual only when its spatial resolution is finer than $h^*\approx 0.7$–$0.9\,\ell$ (where $\ell$ is the boundary injection scale), and $h^*$ grows monotonically with the diffusion length ($0.73 \to 0.93\,\ell$ as $L_D$ ranges $0.05 \to 0.25\,\ell$), so a longer diffusion length smears the boundary signature to a coarser scale that a coarser null already absorbs. The operative distinction of section 7.2 is thereby made precise: a *strong* null is one whose resolution is finer than $h^*(L_D)$. And metabolism earns its stated role numerically — with metabolic maintenance the structured residual is $\approx 20\times$ larger than with the boundary spectrum collapsed toward the smooth background, exactly the function section 7.2 assigns to metabolic expenditure in holding the memory kernel open. None of this makes the boundary real; it makes the null's absorbing power a measurable quantity rather than a rhetorical worry.

## 15. A Low-Dimensional Syntactic-Consistency Demonstration

### 15.1 The Demotion: From "Emergence" to Executable Grammar

The computational demonstration accompanying this architecture must be presented for exactly what it is, and the naive framing must be withdrawn. It does **not** prove that a Lambda-like regime *emerges spontaneously* in any real system, and any claim to that effect is fraudulent. The mode separation, the orthogonal decomposition, and the geometric constraints exhibited in the simulation were *injected by hand into the code*. What the demonstration establishes is therefore strictly bounded: it is a **low-dimensional syntactic-consistency proof** — a demonstration that the grammar's distinctions (projected noise versus free noise, active boundary versus direct coupling, repair-below-threshold versus smooth degradation) are *algorithmically executable* and *non-empty*, that they can be written down as running code that produces the claimed separations. It shows the grammar is not vacuous and not self-contradictory at the level of implementation. It shows nothing about biology. The categories are instantiable; no natural or biological system is shown to instantiate them.

One further admission is required, because the demonstration's very tractability is a liability that an alert reader of the companion paper will exploit. In the toy model, Class G separates from damping (section 15.2) solely by the value of the tangential-diffusion parameter $D_a$ — agency variance $1.02$ versus $0.00$ — with every identity-and-stability metric held identical. This makes the separation a single parametric knob in a low-dimensional state space, and that is precisely the configuration a classical multi-objective stochastic controller can compute, optimize, and track: a Pareto search or a policy-variance-constrained scheme can hold orbital stability while modulating tangential diffusion to a target, reproducing the exact "Class G" trajectory of the toy model. Read naively, the simulation therefore appears to *refute* the companion paper's impossibility claim by handing a controller a direct execution map. This must be conceded without hedging: **in low dimension, with the mode decomposition supplied by the programmer, Class G is not irreducible to control** — a multi-objective controller can track it. The companion paper's impossibility was never a claim about such systems. It concerns the case the toy model cannot represent: high-dimensional biological self-organization in which the $E_{id}\oplus E_{ag}$ decomposition is *not* given in advance by a designer but would have to be carved out by the system's own viability-constrained dynamics, and in which the value structure to be reordered ($J \to J'$) is genuinely novel rather than a pre-enumerated objective. What the controller exploits in the toy model is exactly what the programmer injected: a fixed, low-dimensional, antecedently separated mode structure and a fixed objective family within which "modulate $D_a$" is a well-posed target. Strip those — require the decomposition to emerge from the substrate's own dynamics without external specification, under the full viability constraints of a living cell, against a value structure not in any predefined family — and the controller has neither the coordinates to act on nor a fixed objective to optimize, which is the content of the trilemma restated for the high-dimensional case. The toy model thus demonstrates *consistency* (the categories can be instantiated) at the cost of *tractability* (in low dimension they are controllable); the irreducibility the companion paper argues for is a property of the high-dimensional, self-organizing, viability-constrained regime, not of any system whose mode structure a programmer can hand-separate. Conceding the controllability of the toy model is therefore not a concession about the thesis; it is the precise boundary of what a hand-built low-dimensional model can and cannot show.

### 15.2 The Injected-Mode Construction

The base system realizes the $E_{id}\oplus E_{ag}$ decomposition in the simplest way. A transverse coordinate $r$ measures deviation from an identity manifold $M_\Lambda$ at $r=1$ (the identity-destroying direction); a protected phase $\phi$ carries the identity observable; an agency coordinate $a$, dynamically orthogonal to the identity observable, carries exploratory variation:

$$dr = \lambda_\perp(r-1)\,dt + \sqrt{2D_r}\,dW_r, \qquad d\phi = \omega\,dt + \sqrt{2D_\phi}\,dW_\phi, \qquad da = -\gamma_a a\,dt + \sqrt{2D_a}\,dW_a.$$

The identity observable is $f=\cos\phi$ in the R, P, M regimes and a two-incommensurable-tone observable $f=\cos\phi + \cos(\rho t)$, $\rho$ the golden ratio, in the regimes meant to realize aperiodic retention. This observable is quasi-periodic by construction, which is exactly what is wanted: the demonstration is *not* an attempt to separate Lambda from quasi-periodicity on dynamical grounds (no inspection of a flow can do that without the contract *I*); it uses a quasi-periodic observable only to exhibit retention of correlation power *without strict periodicity*, separating the Lambda-like profile from the exact-recurrence signature of Class P. Each regime is obtained purely by choosing the four parameters $(\lambda_\perp, D_\phi, D_a, \gamma_a)$ — that they are chosen by hand is the whole point of the demotion in section 15.1.

The pre-specified finite-window diagnostics are the correlation-power $P_f = \langle|C_f(t)|^2\rangle$ (persistence), the stationary variance of $a$ (agency-bearing openness, required $>0$), the late-time standard deviation of $r$ (transverse spread), and the maximum autocovariance recurrence at long lags (the P-signature, $\to 1$ for strict periodicity). Averaged over eight noise realizations:

| Regime | $P_f$ (persistence) | agency variance | transverse spread | recurrence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R (random disorder) | 0.003 | 1.02 | 12.12 | 0.08 |
| M (mixing/forgetful) | 0.003 | 1.02 | 1.49 | 0.08 |
| P (mechanical periodicity) | 0.407 | 0.00 | 0.08 | 0.95 |
| Damping (totalizing contraction) | 0.202 | 0.00 | 0.08 | 0.90 |
| Class G (projected noise) | 0.202 | 1.02 | 0.08 | 0.90 |

Three separations are the content. First, R and M collapse persistence to near zero ($P_f\approx 0.003$) while P retains it ($\approx 0.41$): dissolution and forgetting are computationally distinct from rigid retention, and R and M separate on transverse spread (12.12 versus 1.49) rather than persistence. Second — the load-bearing separation — *damping and Class G produce identical persistence, transverse contraction, and recurrence* ($P_f=0.202$, spread $0.08$, recurrence $0.90$) yet differ categorically in agency variance ($0.00$ versus $1.02$): the two interventions are *designed* to be indistinguishable on every identity-and-stability metric and to separate only on the agency axis, which is the executable content of the claim that Class G is not reducible *to damping* — that is, not reducible to the totalizing-contraction strategy of the exogenous horn, which collapses agency to buy stability. This is a distinction from damping specifically, not a claim of irreducibility to control in general; as section 15.1 concedes, in this low dimension and with the mode axes hand-supplied, the $D_a$ separation is itself trackable by a multi-objective controller. The separation shown here is that Class G and damping are different points on the agency axis, not that either is beyond a controller's reach in a toy system. Third, Class G differs from P by keeping agency alive and by being aperiodic. The example is a designed system, not a discovered one: it shows the categories are instantiable, not that any system selects them.

### 15.3 The Active Boundary and the Repair Threshold

Two further injected-construction demonstrations discharge the architectural commitments of this paper in the same restricted sense.

**The active boundary.** A protected macro-variable whose invariant is the phase coherence of a slow oscillator is coupled to a hot classical bath through three architectures: *isolated* (no coupling), *directly coupled* (exposed to thermal backaction), and *$E$-mediated* (coupled only through an active boundary that absorbs backaction while relaying an invariant-conditioned signal). Measuring retention (correlation power) and transduction (a mutual-information proxy), the prediction is that isolation retains but transduces nothing (inert), direct coupling transduces but loses the invariant (thermalizes), and only $E$-mediation achieves both. At low bath temperature this is exactly what the simulation produces, and the $E$-mediated advantage degrades smoothly as temperature rises, vanishing once the bath overwhelms the boundary's finite shielding-and-repair capacity — a computational echo of the thermodynamic bound, consistent with the category correction of section 7.2 (the boundary acts on the substrate's generator with finite metabolic budget). The existence of such a threshold is an architectural prediction; its location in any real system is not fixed by the toy model.

**The repair threshold.** A protected macro-variable equipped with a finite-capacity repair process is perturbed across a sweep of magnitudes. Recording post-perturbation retention yields a sharply thresholded response — retention holds across a plateau then falls abruptly near a critical magnitude — and a sigmoidal (threshold) model fits far better than a linear (smooth-degradation) model, with residual sum of squares smaller by roughly two orders of magnitude. This is the falsifiable signature of dirty-Lambda persistence: degradation by collapse, not proportional decline. The honest qualification is the same throughout: the threshold here follows from a percolation nonlinearity built into the repair rule, so the demonstration shows the architecture is *capable* of producing the signature, not that any biological repair process does. What it removes is the suspicion that "repair threshold" is an empty phrase; what it leaves untouched is the entire empirical question of whether tissue realizes one.

Neither demonstration is evidence about biology, and neither is offered as such. Their function is structural closure: every architectural commitment — projected noise, the active boundary, the repair threshold — is shown to be executable and discriminating, so none rests on suggestion alone. The gap between this architectural logic and any living implementation remains exactly where this paper locates it, to be closed only by model comparison against a measured operational null.

### 15.4 Controlled Failure and the Specificity of Test Two

A further demonstration is reported here precisely because it *fails*, and the failure is more informative than the passes. The qualification tests of section 12 were applied to a macro-pipeline of the kind the architecture envisions: a network of fifty coupled nonlinear (Stuart–Landau) oscillators undergoing a sharp collapse of mean-field coupling gain at a fixed time $t_0$ (a stylized analogue of anesthetic induction); a single projected macro-variable $Q_I$ extracted by principal-component reduction; a deep autoregressive memory kernel ($p=15$) calibrated on the pre-transition regime; and the orthogonal residual $\eta_I$ obtained by removing that kernel's prediction. Test One and Test Two were then run on $\eta_I$ — the deep projected-noise residual, not a hand-constructed signal.

The result is split, and instructively so. Test One passes: the residual undergoes a permanent structural break in local variance at $t_0$ and does not relax to its pre-transition regime, exactly the relaxation asymmetry section 12.1 specifies. Test Two fails: the eigenframe of the residual's delay-embedded covariance does not rotate at $t_0$ (the angular deviation remains within a degree of its baseline), and this failure is robust to sharpening the bifurcation and shifting the post-transition frequency band. The reason is structural and worth stating precisely, because it bounds what Test Two can detect. A delay embedding of a single projected scalar produces a covariance whose principal axis is governed by the signal's autocorrelation at the embedding lag; a collapse of mean-field amplitude or a shift of frequency regime changes the *size* of the resulting phase-space ellipsoid but not the *inclination* of its axes. A genuine, stable eigenvector rotation — the non-holonomic-constraint signature Test Two is meant to detect — requires a distributive reorganization of accessible paths among *multiple genuinely independent degrees of freedom*, which the delayed image of one scalar does not possess. The earlier pass of Test Two on a constructed example (section 15.2's companion demonstrations and the hand-applied rotation used to exhibit the statistic) occurred only because the rotation was *imposed*; when nothing is imposed and the geometry must emerge from a classical amplitude/frequency bifurcation, no rotation appears.

The correct reading of this is specificity, not validation, and the distinction must not be blurred. What the controlled failure establishes is that Test Two is *specific*: a trivial classical phase transition — the mere collapse of a macroscopic amplitude or frequency regime — does not produce its signature, so the test rejects a large and biologically common class of false positives (ordinary bifurcations, amplitude loss, frequency shifts) rather than firing on any dynamical change whatever. What it does *not* establish is the converse: it offers no instance of a system that passes Test Two for a genuine Class-G reason of emergent path reorganization, because the only positive instance available was injected by hand. The existence of a true emergent positive remains undemonstrated. This is the honest state of the matter: section 12's tests jointly exclude linear stochastic noise (section 12.3) and now, by this controlled failure, trivial classical bifurcations as well, which sharpens their specificity — but specificity against false positives is only half of validation, and the other half, an exhibited emergent true positive, is exactly what a low-dimensional hand-built model cannot supply and what this paper does not claim to have shown.

This failure also reinforces, rather than threatens, the shared-generator caution of sections 8.1 and 10.3.2. There, the worry was that a common brainstem drive could produce a Test-One-style variance break in both an orthogonal proxy and the classifier without any identity-bearing boundary being involved. The present result is the same lesson from the other side: an amplitude/frequency collapse driven by a single common generator does produce the Test One break but does *not* produce the Test Two rotation. Test One is therefore the more easily satisfied — and the more easily confounded — of the two, while Test Two's requirement of multivariate path reorganization is the harder bar and the one a shared scalar generator cannot clear on its own. That asymmetry is consistent with making generator-subtracted, multivariate evidence the load-bearing requirement (section 13, rung 5), and with treating a Test One pass alone as necessary but far from sufficient.

### 15.5 Separating an Active Boundary from Self-Organized Criticality: A Demonstration of What Is and Is Not Inabsorbable

Section 12.3 conceded that self-organized criticality (SOC) is the leading non-boundary explanation of structured transitional dynamics and that it passes all three qualification tests, throwing the entire discriminating burden onto out-of-sample prediction against a criticality-complete null. A reviewer is entitled to ask whether that burden can actually be discharged, or whether "active boundary" and "generic criticality" are merely two names for the same residual. This section reports a computational proof of concept built to answer the question — and the answer, developed across the subsections below, is negative for the naive claim and more discriminating than a bare assertion of inabsorbability would suggest. The demonstration is a *syntactic-consistency* result in the sense of section 15.1: it identifies the only candidate separating statistic an additive null cannot absorb, and then shows — by a parameter sweep and by direct tests of the upper rungs — that this statistic, and the rungs meant to back it, fail to separate genuine gating from a bare critical generator at biologically realistic parameters. It establishes that the test is executable and pre-registrable, and that the interaction-coefficient form of the test is invalid; it does not establish positive separability, and it shows nothing about biology.

**The adversarial construction.** The honest worry is that a synthetic boundary engineered to be statistically independent of the critical network would be trivially inabsorbable and would prove nothing about the biological case, where cytoskeletal and membrane structure share energy and feedback with the neural network. The construction therefore enforces strong second-order coupling rather than avoiding it. A probabilistic branching network is run at the critical point (branching parameter $\sigma \approx 1$, the Beggs–Plenz/Kinouchi–Copelli regime), so that it passes the three tests by construction. The synthetic boundary residual $\varepsilon^b_t$ is built as a slow read-out of the network's own mean field plus a minority of private noise, yielding a substantial correlation with the network state (≈ 0.38 against the smoothed mean field in the runs reported), and it feeds back onto the network's local gain. By design, then, $\varepsilon^b_t$ is *highly absorbable as a main effect*: the null is handed maximal additive access to it. The only thing that distinguishes the "boundary" world from a null world is the *form* in which the external perturbation $u_t$ enters: in the boundary (gating) world the perturbation's efficacy is multiplicatively conditioned on the boundary state, $u_t \mapsto g(\varepsilon^b_t)\,u_t$ — receptivity that gates rather than commands — whereas in a matched *placebo* world the perturbation enters additively with the same mean magnitude, $u_t \mapsto \langle g\rangle\,u_t$, carrying no conditional dependence. Everything else — the critical network, the coupling, the feedback, the boundary's own statistics — is identical across the two worlds.

**What turned out to be absorbable (a correction to the naive claim).** The naive expectation was that the explicit interaction regressor $u_t\times\varepsilon^b_t$ would itself be inabsorbable: that adding it to an additive null would raise out-of-sample prediction in the gating world but not the placebo world. This expectation was *falsified* by the demonstration, and the falsification is reported because it is the substantive content. An additive logistic null already containing $\varepsilon^b_t$, $u_t$, and the network state as main effects, augmented by up to fifty-one criticality-derived predictors (multiscale avalanche rate, avalanche-size variance/susceptibility, local branching ratio, and distance-to-criticality $|\sigma-1|$), absorbed the interaction term to within cross-validation noise in *both* worlds: the AUC gain from adding $u_t\times\varepsilon^b_t$ was indistinguishable from zero whether the underlying dynamics were gated or not. The reason is exactly the shared-generator worry made concrete: when the boundary is strongly coupled, its downstream effect on the outcome is partly reconstructable from the network state and the rich criticality features, and a flexible discriminative classifier papers over the missing product. *A linear interaction coefficient in an over-parameterized discriminative model is therefore not a reliable signature of genuine gating.* Had the paper rested its separability claim on that coefficient, the claim would have been wrong.

**What remained inabsorbable, and why.** The signature that did separate the two worlds — robustly, across all five random seeds — is not a regression coefficient but a *stratified conditional-success differential*: the difference between the success rate of perturbations arriving while the boundary is in a receptive state and the success rate of perturbations arriving while it is not, evaluated prospectively (using only pre-perturbation information). In the gating world this differential was positive and bootstrap-significant in every seed (between +0.07 and +0.12, 95% confidence intervals excluding zero); in the placebo world it was null in four of five seeds and a borderline false positive in the fifth. The reason this statistic separates the worlds must be stated carefully, because an earlier and stronger form of the claim — that *no* generative process other than genuine gating can produce the differential — is false and is not what the construction shows. A strictly *additive* model of perturbation success, in which $u_t$ and $\varepsilon^b_t$ contribute separately to the transition probability, cannot produce a dependence of the perturbation's marginal effect on the boundary state, because in such a model that effect is by construction constant across boundary states; the conditional-success differential measures precisely that forbidden dependence, and no amount of *additive* criticality predictors reconstructs it, because they enter as main effects on the baseline rate, not as modulators of the perturbation slope. But the live competitor is not additive — it is a *nonlinear* critical generator, and near criticality the susceptibility (dynamic range) of such a generator is itself state-dependent (Kinouchi & Copelli 2006), so in principle a critical generator's perturbation efficacy *can* covary with a mean-field read-out like $\varepsilon^b_t$ without any active boundary. The construction's actual finding is therefore empirical and conditional, not a priori: in a faithful stochastic branching network at $\sigma\approx1$, that intrinsic susceptibility-driven dependence is *swamped by the heavy-tailed variance of critical avalanches* and does not produce a detectable placebo differential at the coupling and correlation of the noisy-read-out regime, whereas a multiplicative gate on the perturbation itself produces a differential large enough to survive that variance. The separating power of the differential, *in that regime*, is thus that it isolates a *large multiplicative modulation of the perturbation* from the *modest, variance-dominated susceptibility modulation* a bare critical generator supplies. This separation is, however, regime-specific rather than categorical, and the next paragraph shows it does not hold generically: at a clean read-out and weakly homeostatic feedback the critical susceptibility modulation is no longer modest, and the placebo differential matches or exceeds the gating one at the realistic correlation. Resolving the criticality dynamics more finely raises the baseline AUC but leaves the differential essentially where it is, because finer additive predictors capture the baseline rate, not the perturbation-slope modulation.

**The three lessons, stated without inflation.** First, the naive separability claim does *not* survive: while there is an executable, pre-registrable statistic — the stratified conditional-success differential — that an additive null cannot reproduce, that statistic does not by itself distinguish a genuinely gated system from a matched non-gated critical one, because (as the parameter sweep above and the upper-rung tests below show) a bare nonlinear critical generator reproduces it at realistic parameters. The reviewer's challenge that "active boundary" and "generic criticality" might be two names for the same residual is therefore *not* met by this construction. Second, the interaction coefficient in a flexible classifier is the wrong statistic regardless: it is absorbable under strong coupling and must not be used, so Section 14's interaction-model comparison ($M_A$ versus $M_G$) is demoted to corroborating evidence at most. Third, whatever discriminating power the architecture can claim requires a *manipulable* perturbation $u_t$ whose timing relative to the boundary state can be observed — the perturbational-tractability condition of section 3.3 and the boundary-gating requirement of rung 6 — since without intervention there is no differential to measure at all, and a purely observational residual remains on the criticality side of the line. The proof of concept therefore does *not* convert the section 1.2 positive criterion into an exhibited working detector; it exhibits the statistic the criterion would use and then shows that statistic, and the upper rungs meant to back it, fail to separate gating from criticality in this model (next paragraph). What it establishes positively is narrow: the test is executable and the interaction-coefficient form is invalid. It shows nothing about biology, and it does not show that any tissue realizes — or that this protocol could detect — the gating it set out to demonstrate.

**A dependence the construction does not control, stated as a limitation.** The placebo-null result reported above was reproduced in an independent faithful reimplementation of the network, but only inside a specific corner of parameter space, and a two-dimensional sweep over the feedback strength and the private-noise level shows the null is *not* generic. Three control parameters that the original construction leaves unspecified jointly determine whether a bare critical generator produces the stratified differential: (i) the sign of the $\varepsilon^b_t\to$gain feedback, (ii) the magnitude of the private noise in $\varepsilon^b_t$ (equivalently, how clean the boundary read-out is), and (iii) the polarity of what $\varepsilon^b_t$ reads off the mean field. The findings of the sweep are as follows. The clean placebo-null holds only for *neutral-to-positive* feedback combined with a *noisy* read-out, the regime in which the correlation ($\approx 0.38$) happens to sit. As the read-out is cleaned (private noise lowered) and the feedback made weakly *homeostatic* (negative) — both biologically natural choices, since real boundary regulation is predominantly homeostatic and a well-resolved proxy is the goal of measurement — the placebo differential becomes large, significant across seeds, and present *at the realistic correlation*: at a realized $\varepsilon^b$–mean-field correlation of $\approx 0.42$ under weak homeostatic feedback, the bare-critical placebo differential reached $\approx -0.25$ with bootstrap confidence intervals excluding zero in every seed — larger in magnitude than the $+0.07$ to $+0.12$ gating differential it is meant to be distinguished from. Two would-be defenses are themselves weak. Sign is not a reliable discriminant: the critical leak is negative when $\varepsilon^b_t$ tracks activity but *positive* when it tracks availability, so a critical generator can match the positive polarity of genuine gating by the choice of read-out alone. Magnitude is not a reliable discriminant either, since the leak can exceed the gating signal. The honest statement is therefore stronger than "the placebo cannot produce the differential": a bare nonlinear critical generator *can* produce a stratified conditional-success differential at the realistic correlation, of either sign and of comparable or larger magnitude than the gating signature, whenever the boundary read-out is clean and the feedback weakly homeostatic. The reported null-placebo is a real but *non-generic* result, contingent on unstated and not-obviously-biological assumptions about feedback sign and read-out noise. What this does *not* overturn: the stratified differential is only one rung of the cumulative G-admissibility criterion (§14), and the present sweep tests that rung alone. Whether the further requirements — directional barrier-lowering toward the $\Lambda$-like target, orthogonal macro-invariant preservation in the generator-subtracted residual (§8.1), and cross-preparation generalization — still separate genuine gating from a critical leak that mimics the differential is not established here and is the live question on which the positive criterion now turns. A pre-registered application must accordingly (a) fix and report the assumed feedback sign and read-out-noise level, (b) treat the stratified differential as necessary but far from sufficient, and (c) require the directional and generalization rungs to carry the discriminating load, since the differential alone is reproducible by non-gated criticality at realistic parameters.

**Do the upper rungs rescue the discrimination? A direct test, and a negative result for this construction.** Because the load was just shifted onto the directional and generalization rungs, those rungs were themselves put under the leak regime ($\beta\approx-0.05$, clean read-out) and tested directly against a bare-critical placebo. Neither, as instantiated in this proof-of-concept network, separates the leak from genuine gating. The *directional* rung asks whether receptive-state perturbation lowers the barrier in a *sustained, directed* way toward a $\Lambda$-like target rather than producing a larger transient avalanche that relaxes back. Measured as the differential in mean-field displacement at long lag ($L=80$ steps), the sustained component was negligible in both worlds — of order $-0.002$ against an immediate-avalanche differential of $0.13$–$0.41$ — and, critically, *identical in the gating and placebo worlds* (both $\approx-0.002$, same sign, confidence intervals overlapping). The reason is structural: modelling gating as a multiplicative rescaling of transient perturbation efficacy, as this construction does, opens no persistent attractor the prior dynamics lacked, so the gating world has no sustained directed shift for a directional rung to detect. The proof-of-concept's gating model and the architecture's own directional criterion are thus *misaligned*: the former cannot exhibit the property the latter requires, so the directional rung is untestable as a discriminator here and would separate the leak only if genuine biological gating opened a sustained attractor — a property that must be demonstrated with a structural-boundary mechanism, not the efficacy modulation used here. The *generalization* rung (Rung 7) fared no better, and here the result is clean rather than merely inconclusive: varying the preparation — network degree $k\in\{4,10,20\}$, random versus ring-regular topology, and distributed versus localized stimulation — left the bare-critical leak signature essentially invariant (negative sign in every preparation and every seed, magnitude stable at $\approx-0.16$, attenuating only to $-0.09$ on the ring lattice, significant throughout). This is exactly what critical universality (Kinouchi & Copelli 2006) predicts: susceptibility at $\sigma\approx1$ is a universal property, not a topological accident, so the leak generalizes across preparations as robustly as a structural boundary property would. The generalization rung therefore does not unmask the leak. The honest consequence is that *none* of the three rungs — stratified differential, directional barrier-lowering, cross-preparation generalization — separates a bare critical generator from genuine gating *in this proof-of-concept model*. This is a failure of the specific instantiation offered as evidence of feasibility, not a proof that no instantiation can succeed; but it inverts the burden. The §15.5 construction was meant to exhibit positive feasibility for the eliminative protocol, and instead it demonstrates that its own gating model cannot satisfy its own upper criteria and that its lowest criterion is mimicked by criticality. A future feasibility demonstration must therefore (a) model gating as a genuine structural boundary that opens a persistent attractor the ungated dynamics lacks — so that the directional rung has sustained directed displacement to detect — rather than as multiplicative efficacy modulation, and (b) show that the gating signature survives a preparation change in a way the universal critical susceptibility does not. Until such a construction exists, the positive arm of CBRA should be regarded as *not yet demonstrated to be feasible*, and the architecture's defensible contribution is its eliminative/negative arm (ruling out gating when no differential survives), not a positive detector of agency-bearing boundary organization.

## 16. Relationship to Consciousness and Agency

Consciousness relevance is downstream, not a central commitment. CBRA is primarily a theory of biological persistence conditions; whether those conditions contribute to phenomenal experience requires a separate argument this paper does not make. CBRA does not identify $Q_I$ with the soul, microtubules with consciousness, or any state with subjective experience. To become consciousness-relevant, the architecture would have to satisfy three additional mutual-information requirements simultaneously,

$$\mathcal{I}(E_t; W_t) > 0, \qquad \mathcal{I}(E_t; \Pi_{t:t+\tau}) > 0, \qquad \mathcal{I}(E_t; R_t) > 0,$$

where $W_t$ is a global-workspace or integrative state, $\Pi$ a policy distribution, and $R_t$ self-model integration (Metzinger 2003). Boundary-mediated structure that does not couple to global availability, policy, or self-modeling is architecturally relevant but not consciousness-relevant.

## 17. Limitations and Boundary Conditions

Several limitations are internal to the architecture. First, CBRA is a *conditional* research architecture: it becomes empirically interesting only if boundary-state variables are independently measurable, technologically accessible, physiologically relevant, causally transductive, structurally qualified (including the three tests of section 12), and non-redundant under operational model comparison. Second, the contract *I* remains domain-relative; the architecture constrains its use but does not supply a universal identity criterion. Third, the cardio-interoceptive material is *not* a theory of cardiac consciousness: heart-brain coupling, HRV, HEPs, and baroreflex dynamics support a role for the heart in embodied *regulation* and as a *receptivity boundary*, not as a cognitive subject or seat of identity, and Path C fails if cardiac variables are redundant with the null. A further limitation specific to Path C is that the orthogonal-preservation test on cardiac proxies secures measurement dissociation, not causal independence: because cardiac and interoceptive dynamics are coupled to the same central generators that drive the transitions being classified — most acutely during anesthetic emergence — a clean statistical pass cannot exclude that the proxy and the classifier are two readouts of one process, and Path C results must be reported under that bound (section 10.3.2). A direct simulation audit (section 8.1) sharpens this from a qualitative caution into a quantitative precondition: when the shared generator acts *nonlinearly*, the generator-subtracted-residual test false-positives at a rate that *grows with record length* and is controlled only when the generator proxy captures $\gtrsim 95\%$ of its variance — a fidelity the latent neuromodulatory generators of Path C are unlikely to permit, so a Rung-5 pass on current measurements cannot carry the discriminating load. Fourth, the computational material establishes executability and non-vacuity only; it is never biological evidence.

Fifth, and most consequentially for the architecture's empirical prospects: the leading non-boundary explanation of structured transitional cortical dynamics is self-organized criticality, which generically reproduces every signature CBRA can detect — heavy-tailed nonlinearity, regime change, and eigenframe reorganization (section 12.3). CBRA has independent content only if a boundary residual predicts an $I$-supporting transition after a *criticality-complete* null is included, and only if the boundary-gating interaction and orthogonal preservation hold jointly (the resolution-independent positive criterion of section 1.2). If criticality predictors absorb the residual, CBRA collapses into a redescription of critical dynamics, and the architecture treats this as one of its primary failure modes rather than a confound to be argued around.

A boundary condition concerns model rupture and its interpretation. A structured failure of the operational null is, by default, a model-discrepancy signal — never a measurement of anything beyond unmodeled physiology until the full adversarial sequence and the three tests have been passed, and even then only a *statistical* case for an organizationally maintained transition. CBRA must not be read as a detector of anything metaphysical: its role is limited to specifying what continuity conditions an embodied system would have to preserve *if* an *I*-indexed macro-invariant were maintained through transformation, and it can neither model nor infer the cause of any such transformation.

## 18. Conclusion

This paper has specified a strict eliminative statistical protocol for a single conditional question: *if* an identity-indexed, Lambda-like persistence channel were materially realized in living tissue, what would the tissue have to implement, and what residual structure would survive the strongest available operational null model? The answer is a model-comparison architecture — from a pre-specified identity contract, through a projected macro-variable, an active boundary, a measured boundary vector, and a structurally qualified residual, to prospective out-of-sample prediction of a target transition — whose load-bearing element is the preservation of a macro-invariant *through* the transition by an orthogonal, independently measured proxy, the only point at which the protocol distinguishes a preserved trajectory from a reconstructed match. That load-bearing element is itself bounded by a measurement-fidelity condition established in section 8.1: against a nonlinearly acting common generator the generator-subtracted-residual test is informative only when the generator is measured at high fidelity (proxy variance-capture $\gtrsim 95\%$), and below that threshold it false-positives at a rate that grows with record length — a condition the architecture's flagship application (anesthetic emergence, with its latent neuromodulatory generator) is unlikely to meet. The protocol's secure contribution is therefore eliminative, and even its load-bearing positive rung carries a stated fidelity precondition rather than an unconditional warrant.

Four corrections separate this architecture from its naive predecessor. The Mori–Zwanzig category error is removed: the physical boundary does not interact with the projection operator but belongs to the substrate $C\cup E$ and acts on its Liouville generator before projection, sustaining the memory kernel against collapse through metabolic expenditure. The indefensible claim that cardiac variables are constitutive invariants is withdrawn under the heart-transplant constraint, and the reclassification of those variables as receptivity-boundary proxies turns the transplant from a refutation into a confirmation of the framework's constitutive/boundary partition. The computational demonstration is demoted to a low-dimensional syntactic-consistency proof — executable and non-empty, never evidence of spontaneous emergence or biological actuality. And the residual-qualification layer is strengthened with three explicit tests — relaxation asymmetry, idempotent covariance rotation, and multivariate IAAFT surrogate testing — that jointly defeat the central neuroscientific objection that any structured residual is mere long-tailed background fluctuation.

CBRA does not claim that the brain implements such a channel, and it is built so that a clean negative result is possible at every rung of its validation ladder. Its contribution is to convert a conditional question into a falsifiable protocol: not evidence that living tissue maintains an identity-bearing invariant through transition, but a precise and rejectable specification of the empirical burden any such claim must discharge, and the many ways it fails. The architecture's value lies entirely in being easy to reject — and in surviving rejection, if it does, only against the strongest operational competitor and the strictest residual controls available.

## Appendix A — Suspended Sub-Molecular Implementation Branches

This appendix collects the sub-molecular mechanisms sometimes proposed as physical realizers of a protected biological invariant — spin-selective electron transport (CISS), radical-pair and cryptochrome dynamics, and nuclear-spin isotope branches. They are placed outside the main argument for a deliberate reason: nothing in the protocol depends on any of them. The functional core — an actively maintained boundary that protects a macro-invariant against destructive diffusion, repairs perturbation below threshold, and transduces boundary-conditioned structure into classical dynamics non-redundantly against a strong operational null — can be tested, and can fail, entirely through the cytoskeletal (Paths A/B) and cardio-interoceptive (Path C) routes. The branches here may be rejected in full without weakening that core.

**The thermal-scale problem.** Under physiologically plausible field strengths, the relevant spin (Zeeman) energy scale is many orders of magnitude smaller than the thermal energy $k_B T$ at body temperature. Any spin-dependent signal would have to survive and be amplified across a very large energy gap before coupling to neural-scale dynamics. Whether the required cumulative amplification across molecular, channel, network, and state levels is physically available is an open quantitative question this paper does not settle (Gammaitoni et al. 1998). Until quantitative amplification, co-localization, and functional neural transduction are demonstrated at physiologically relevant scales, these branches do not contribute to the empirical program.

**Why suspended rather than asserted.** Each branch is a high-risk candidate realization of a spin-sensitive $Q_I$ and can fail independently — CISS as a directionality mechanism, cryptochrome or TRPC1 as a membrane-transduction path, nuclear-spin isotope effects by scale, thermal noise, co-localization, amplification, or sheer irrelevance to the *I*-constitutive invariant. The architecture treats these as the most demanding and least conservative options precisely so that CBRA cannot survive by quietly migrating $Q_I$ from a failed conservative interpretation to an exotic one after the fact. The conservative realizations — correlational, topological, and boundary-state-ensemble — remain the default.

**Relation to quantum-mind proposals.** Suspending these branches marks a boundary against a common misreading. CBRA is not a defense of Orchestrated Objective Reduction or related quantum-consciousness proposals (Hameroff and Penrose 2014); it does not require quantum coherence and is not vulnerable to the standard decoherence objections (Tegmark 2000; Zurek 2003), because its functional core is classical. A reader who regards all quantum-biological proposals as implausible loses nothing in the main argument by discarding this appendix.

**A note on the no-cloning analogy.** The companion paper's treatment of resurrection and duplication turns on the claim that singular personal identity is not secured by copyable information. A structural analogy from physics is worth recording in the same suspended register: the no-cloning theorem establishes that an arbitrary unknown quantum state cannot be copied by any physical process (Wootters and Zurek 1982). Its relevance is purely structural — it shows that physically non-copyable information is a feature some well-defined quantities provably possess, removing the suspicion that non-replicable singularity is incoherent in principle. No quantum claim about personal identity is made.

## Appendix B — Computational Verification and Replication Benchmarks

This appendix documents the seven computational checks used to verify that the qualification protocol of section 12 and the model-comparison conditions of sections 3 and 8.1 are *executable, well-posed, and reproducible across independent environments*. The appendix is held to the same standard as section 15: none of these checks is evidence that any biological system implements a Lambda-like channel. Experiments 1–3 operate on a real electrophysiological signal and verify that the detection algorithms behave as specified; experiments 4–5 operate on synthetic data and verify that the model-comparison inequalities are well-posed; experiment 6 is the controlled failure of section 15.4; experiment 7 is the self-organized-criticality separability proof of concept of section 15.5. What the appendix establishes is replication of *method*, not confirmation of *hypothesis*, and the divergences between environments are reported as fully as the agreements, because a replication record that hides its disagreements is worthless.

**Environment.** All checks use Python 3 with `numpy`, `scipy`, `scikit-learn`, and, for the electrophysiological signal, `neurokit2` (v0.2.13). The real-data experiments draw the first 2,500 samples of the `ECG` channel of the `bio_resting_5min_100hz` dataset distributed with NeuroKit2; that dataset is deterministic and bit-identical across installations, which is why the real-data checks replicate tightly. The synthetic experiments depend on a pseudo-random seed; absent a shared seed, only the *direction and significance* of their results are expected to coincide across environments, not the third-decimal values. Two independent runs are compared throughout: a reference run (Colab) and a verification run (a separate Linux environment).

**B.1 Experiment 1 — IAAFT surrogate test (real ECG).** *Purpose:* test the null that the signal's structure is linear stochastic noise (section 12.3). *Method:* 1,000 iterative amplitude-adjusted Fourier-transform surrogates, each preserving the amplitude distribution and power spectrum while randomizing higher-order phase structure; a time-reversal-asymmetry statistic is computed on the real series and on each surrogate. *Benchmark (reference):* the real series rejects the null at $p<0.001$, falling far outside the surrogate confidence band. *Verification run:* $p=0.001$ (the empirical floor for 1,000 surrogates), real statistic isolated in the left tail at $z\approx-34$ relative to the surrogate ensemble. *Concordance:* the decision is identical — real signal nonlinear, null rejected at the 99.9% level. The numerical discrepancy in the reported deviation (reference $\approx-4.8$ versus verification $z\approx-34$) is a consequence of the asymmetry statistic's normalization (the denominator scaling), not of the test logic; both environments place the real signal decisively outside the linear-surrogate band. *Status:* replicated (decision); statistic-definition-dependent in magnitude.

**B.2 Experiment 2 — Test One, relaxation asymmetry (CUSUM).** *Purpose:* verify detection of a permanent, non-relaxing structural break (section 12.1). *Method:* a persistent local-variance perturbation is injected into the standardized ECG from $t_0=1000$; a 50-sample moving variance is tracked and a single-shift change-point statistic (normalized CUSUM) locates the break. *Benchmark:* break near $t_0=1000$, local variance stepping from $\approx1.0$ to the $2.0$–$3.5$ band and holding, with no relaxation to baseline. *Verification run:* break detected at index $1029$; pre-$t_0$ variance $0.96$, post-$t_0$ $2.80$, final-window $2.92$ (no relaxation). *Concordance:* tight — the deterministic dataset and the specified injection produce matching values. One implementation note is recorded for reproducers: a raw cumulative-sum `argmax` lands at the series end for a *sustained* level shift; the correct single-shift estimator normalizes the cumulative deviation by $\sqrt{k(n-k)/n}$, which recovers the break at $1029$. *Status:* replicated.

**B.3 Experiment 3 — Test Two, covariance-eigenframe rotation.** *Purpose:* verify detection of a discrete geometric reorientation, the imposed-constraint signature (section 12.2). *Method:* a 2-D delay embedding (lag $\tau=5$) of the ECG is subjected to a genuine $45°$ orthogonal rotation from $t_0=1000$; the leading-eigenvector angle of a trailing-window local covariance is tracked against the pre-$t_0$ reference frame. *Benchmark:* a step from $\approx11°$ to $\approx46°$. *Verification run:* $10.8°\to46.6°$. *Concordance:* near-exact. The reproduction note: a *trailing* window (length $\sim150$) is required for a stable baseline; a centered window straddling $t_0$ inflates the pre-transition angle and smears the step. *Status:* replicated. Note that the rotation here is *imposed*, not emergent — this is the constructed positive whose emergent counterpart experiment 6 fails to find (section 15.4).

**B.4 Experiment 4 — out-of-sample non-redundancy (rungs 4–5).** *Purpose:* verify that the non-redundancy inequality of section 3 is well-posed and that an informative boundary residual improves held-out prediction. *Method:* synthetic operational predictors $N^{op}$ plus a channel-noised boundary residual $\varepsilon^b$ that genuinely contributes to the outcome; two L2-penalized logistic models ($M_0:N^{op}$; $M_1:N^{op}+\varepsilon^b$) compared by 5-fold cross-validated ROC-AUC. *Benchmark:* $M_0\;0.7293 \to M_1\;0.7677$ (gain $+0.038$). *Verification run:* $M_0\;0.697 \to M_1\;0.763$ (gain $+0.066$); notably $M_1$ nearly coincides across environments. *Concordance:* directional and near-quantitative; the residual adds robust out-of-sample value in both runs. The absolute-level offset is the expected consequence of an unshared seed and unshared data-generating coefficients. *Status:* replicated (direction and significance).

**B.5 Experiment 5 — biological Class G admissibility (rung 6).** *Purpose:* verify that the gating interaction of section 8.1 is well-posed and separable from additive forcing. *Method:* synthetic data in which a binary intervention $u_t$ acts under a multiplicative (boundary-gated) rule on a receptivity state $\varepsilon^b$; an additive model $M_A:[N^{op},u,\varepsilon^b]$ is compared to the interaction model $M_G:[N^{op},u,\varepsilon^b,u\!\times\!\varepsilon^b]$ by cross-validated AUC. *Benchmark:* $M_A\;0.7257 \to M_G\;0.7616$ (gain $+0.036$). *Verification run:* $M_A\;0.675 \to M_G\;0.729$ (gain $+0.055$). *Concordance:* directional; the multiplicative coupling term adds genuine predictive power in both, confirming that boundary-gating is distinguishable from additive forcing *against a plain additive null*. This narrow well-posedness does not survive a criticality-complete null: Experiment 7 (B.7) shows the same interaction-coefficient gain is absorbed once rich criticality predictors are included, which is why the AUC-gain signature is demoted (sections 14, 15.5) and only the stratified differential is retained — itself shown non-diagnostic at realistic parameters. The consistent $\sim0.03$–$0.05$ absolute offset across experiments 4 and 5 again reflects seed and coefficient differences, not a method discrepancy. *Status:* replicated (direction and significance), with the signature demoted under the stronger null of B.7.

**B.6 Experiment 6 — Mori–Zwanzig macro-pipeline (controlled failure).** *Purpose:* run Tests One and Two on an *emergent* deep residual, with no hand-injected geometry (section 15.4). *Method:* fifty coupled Stuart–Landau oscillators undergo a collapse of mean-field coupling gain at $t_0=1000$; a principal-component reduction extracts the macro-variable $Q_I$; a $p=15$ autoregressive memory kernel calibrated pre-transition yields the orthogonal residual $\eta_I$; Tests One and Two are applied to $\eta_I$. *Benchmark (reference):* both tests pass — "QUALIFIED." *Verification run:* Test One passes (permanent variance break, no relaxation); Test Two **fails** (eigenframe angular deviation $\approx0.5°\to0.6°$, robust to sharpening the bifurcation and shifting the post-transition frequency). *Concordance:* partial, and the divergence is the appendix's most important entry. The verification environment cannot reproduce a Test-Two pass without injecting a rotation by hand, because a delay embedding of a single projected scalar has no geometric freedom to rotate under a mere amplitude/frequency collapse (section 15.4). The reference "QUALIFIED" result therefore most likely reflects different oscillator/coupling constants or a different embedding that produces a genuine multivariate reorientation; pinning the exact source requires the reference run's constants and seed. *Status:* Test One replicated; Test Two divergent — and the divergence is interpreted (section 15.4) as evidence of Test Two's *specificity* against trivial classical bifurcations, not as a defect. A reproducer who obtains a Test-Two pass on an emergent (non-injected) residual should treat that as a finding requiring scrutiny, not as routine confirmation.

**How experiments 5 and 7 fit together.** Experiment 5 reports that the interaction term $u\times\varepsilon^b$ adds out-of-sample value over an additive model; experiment 7 reports that the same term adds *nothing* once the null is a strongly-coupled criticality model. The two are consistent and their difference is the whole lesson. Experiment 5 uses a weak synthetic null (no criticality predictors) and a boundary only loosely coupled to the substrate, so the interaction coefficient is identifiable. Experiment 7 enforces the adversarial conditions section 12.3 demands — a critical generator, strong second-order coupling, and a criticality-complete additive null — and under those conditions the interaction *coefficient* becomes absorbable, while the *stratified conditional-success differential* does not. Experiment 5 is therefore retained only as a well-posedness check (the inequality computes), and its interpretation is governed by experiment 7: the operative test is the stratified differential under a placebo contrast, not the interaction-term AUC gain (sections 14, 15.5).

**B.7 Experiment 7 — active boundary versus self-organized criticality (separability proof of concept).** *Purpose:* test whether a genuinely boundary-gated system is distinguishable from a matched non-gated system when the competitor is a critical network and the null is criticality-complete (sections 12.3, 15.5). *Method:* a probabilistic branching network at $\sigma\approx1$ (Beggs–Plenz/Kinouchi–Copelli regime); a synthetic boundary residual $\varepsilon^b_t$ built as a slow read-out of the network mean field plus minority private noise (correlation $\approx0.38$ with the smoothed mean field) with feedback onto network gain; an external perturbation $u_t$ entering multiplicatively as $g(\varepsilon^b_t)\,u_t$ in the *gating* world and additively as $\langle g\rangle\,u_t$ in a matched *placebo* world. Two statistics are compared across both worlds and across an additive null augmented with up to 51 criticality predictors (multiscale avalanche rate, susceptibility, branching ratio, distance-to-criticality): (a) the AUC gain from adding the regressor $u_t\times\varepsilon^b_t$; (b) the prospective stratified conditional-success differential (receptive vs non-receptive boundary) with bootstrap confidence intervals. *Result:* statistic (a) is indistinguishable from zero in *both* worlds at every null complexity — the interaction coefficient is absorbed. Statistic (b) is positive and bootstrap-significant in the gating world across all five seeds ($+0.069$ to $+0.122$, 95% CIs excluding zero) and null in the placebo world in four of five seeds *at the noisy-read-out parameters* (neutral-to-positive feedback, noisy read-out, correlation $\approx0.38$). *Status (section 15.5):* this placebo-null is *not generic*. A two-dimensional parameter sweep shows that under a clean read-out and weakly homeostatic feedback — both biologically natural — a bare critical generator reproduces statistic (b) at the realistic correlation, with magnitude exceeding the gating signal and sign set by read-out convention; and direct tests of the two upper rungs (directional barrier-lowering, cross-preparation generalization) fail to separate the critical leak from gating in this model. The separability claim of section 12.3 is therefore *not* supported by this construction: statistic (a) is an unreliable signature and is demoted, and statistic (b), though inabsorbable by an additive null, is reproducible by non-gated criticality at realistic parameters. This remains a syntactic-consistency result (section 15.1) — the test is executable and pre-registrable — but it establishes the *failure* of the proof of concept to exhibit positive separability, not its success; no tissue realizes, and this protocol is not shown to detect, the gating. Two independent runs agree on direction and significance; absolute differential magnitudes are seed-dependent as expected for synthetic data.

The pattern is the honest one to report: deterministic real-data checks replicate tightly (experiments 1–3), seed-dependent synthetic checks replicate in direction and significance with absolute-level offsets (experiments 4–5), and the one check designed to probe an *emergent* geometric signature diverges in exactly the place section 15.4 predicts it should be hard to satisfy (experiment 6). No check is offered as biological evidence; together they establish that the protocol is executable, well-posed, and reproducible as *method*, and that its hardest test (Test Two on an emergent residual) is specific enough to fail on a trivial bifurcation — which is the property an eliminative protocol must have.
