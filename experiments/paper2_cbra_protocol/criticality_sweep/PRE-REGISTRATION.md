# Experiment I — Robustness of the Criticality Confound (Paper 2)

**Run order: 9th.**

## Question
Paper 2 (Sec. 12.3 / 15.5) already concedes that a bare nonlinear critical
generator reproduces the stratified conditional-success differential the gating
test relies on, at realistic parameters. Is that damaging conclusion **robust**
across a wider family of critical/near-critical generators, or is it fragile to
parameter variation (which would partially rescue the test)?

## Method
Broaden the family of critical / self-organized-critical generators tested against
the stratified conditional differential: more combinations of coupling strength,
read-out noise, and homeostatic-feedback strength. For each, check whether the
critical generator still reproduces the separating statistic (magnitude and sign)
that an additive generative process cannot.

## Pre-registered success criterion (understood as: confound is robust)
If the critical generator reproduces the differential across most of the broadened
parameter space, the Sec. 15.5 concession is **robust** and the negative
(eliminative) framing of CBRA stands as the defensible arm.

## Pre-registered failure criterion (confound is fragile)
If there is a substantial, realistic parameter region where the critical generator
**fails** to reproduce the differential, then the gating test is not as thoroughly
confounded as Sec. 15.5 states, and the paper's concession may be **too strong** —
report the region where the detection arm might survive.

## Stopping rule
One pre-fixed generator family and parameter grid. Either direction is a publishable
correction; do not stop early on the first confirming generator.

## Status
Pre-registered. Not yet implemented.
